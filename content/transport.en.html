Seoul Transportation & Navigation Guide — Move Around Like a Local

At a Glance

Item Details
Easiest Transport Subway (지하철; 8 lines, ubiquitous, English signage)
Cheapest Option Bus (₩1,250 flat rate)
Fastest Option Taxi (avoid rush hours)
Most Reliable Subway (runs until 11-12 PM)
Must-Have T-money (티머니) card (reloadable transit card)
Navigation App Naver Maps (네이버 지도) or KakaoMap (카카오맵) (superior to Google Maps)
Difficulty Level Easy (all systems designed for foreigners)

Why Seoul Transportation Matters

"It's a Transport City"

  • Integrated System: Subway + bus + taxi form seamless network
  • Speed: Cross entire city in 45 minutes (vs. 2+ hours driving)
  • Affordability: Single trip ₩1,250~2,500 (~$0.89~$1.79 USD)
  • Coverage: Reaches 99% of tourist destinations
  • Safety: Zero harassment, women-only subway cars available

Seoul Subway System (지하철)

Overview

Lines: 9 main lines + 7 branch lines + light rail (extensive coverage)

Hours: 5:30 AM~11 PM (weekdays), 5:30 AM~12 AM (weekends)

Frequency: Trains every 3-5 minutes (peak hours)

English: Full English signage, announcements, app support

How to Use the Subway

Step 1: Get a T-Money Card

What It Is: T-money (티머니) — reloadable transit card accepted at subway, bus, taxis, convenience stores.

Where to Buy:

  • GS25, CU, Emart24 convenience stores
  • Subway station kiosks
  • Airport arrival (multiple locations)

Cost: ₩2,500~4,000 (card itself) + reload amount (₩10,000~50,000)

How to Load: Swipe at convenience store, kiosk, or reload machine; speak to cashier: "충전해주세요" (Charge please)

Step 2: Enter Subway Station

Process:

1. Find nearest Line 1-9 entrance (look for subway symbol/sign)

2. Tap T-money card on reader (sound = registered)

3. Walk through gate

4. Follow signs to your line (English signs everywhere)

Step 3: Find Your Platform

Signs Show:

  • Line number + color
  • Station name (Korean + English)
  • Train direction (Express/Local)

Timing: Check electronic display for next train (usually 2-5 min wait)

Step 4: Board Train

Etiquette:

  • Let passengers exit first
  • Stand aside for elderly/pregnant/disabled
  • Women-only cars (front/back) = all-female, peace of mind
  • Hold handrail (trains stop suddenly)

Step 5: Get Off at Your Station

Audio Announcement: English + Korean announcement 1 station before arrival

Process:

1. Watch electronic display

2. Press exit button when arriving

3. Follow exit signs (color-coded, bilingual)

4. Tap card on exit reader (automatic charge)

Subway Fares

Regular Fare: ₩1,250 (first 10 km)

Distance-Based Fare:

  • 10-20 km: ₩1,450
  • 20-50 km: ₩1,850
  • 50+ km: ₩2,250

Day Pass: ₩7,000 (unlimited travel 24 hours; worthwhile if making 6+ trips)

T-Money vs. Cash: T-money slightly cheaper (₩50 discount per trip)

Seoul Bus System (버스)

Overview

Routes: 400+ routes citywide

Types: Regular buses (파랑), Blue buses (간선; limited stops, faster), Red buses (광역; long-distance)

Frequency: Every 5-15 minutes (popular routes)

Hours: 5:30 AM~11 PM (routes vary)

How to Use the Bus

Step 1: Identify Bus Number

Format: Numbers (1-4 digits) with color-coded signs

Busiest Numbers:

  • 1-100 (Local buses, neighborhood coverage)
  • 100-500 (Inter-district buses)
  • 500+ (Long-distance express buses)

Step 2: Wait at Bus Stop

Stop Signs: Clearly marked; check route map (often has English)

Safety: Bus stops well-lit, safe, busy

Step 3: Board & Tap Card

Process:

1. Signal bus to stop (wave if driver doesn't see you)

2. Board front door

3. Tap T-money card on reader (front of bus)

4. Proceed to seat

Step 4: Get Off

Signal: Press bell button (red button, located on poles) before stop

Exit: Use rear door

Tap Again: Some buses require second tap at exit (announcement says); if unsure, tap once only

Bus Etiquette

Behavior OK?
Eating ⭐⭐⭐ (Common)
Loud talking ⭐⭐ (Discouraged but happens)
Phone calls ⭐⭐ (Brief calls OK; long calls bad)
Standing in aisle ⭐⭐⭐ (Normal, no seats)

Bus Fares

Regular Fare: ₩1,250 (unlimited distance!)

Express Bus (고속버스): ₩2,450

One-Day Pass: ₩4,500 (unlimited bus only; subway separate)

Bus vs. Subway Decision Matrix

Taxis (택시)

Overview

Types: Regular taxi (silver/beige, most common), Deluxe taxi (black, 25% premium price)

Cost: ₩3,000 base + ₩100 per 142m + ₩30 per 35 seconds (waiting time)

Typical Trip: 5 km = ₩12,000~15,000

Availability: 24/7, abundant

Payment: Cash or card (card has ₩1,000 minimum charge)

When to Use Taxi

Situation Recommended
Arrived late (after 11 PM subway closure) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Heavy luggage/shopping bags ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Unfamiliar destination, complex route ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Bad weather (heavy rain, snow) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Solo woman late night ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Exact address hard to navigate ⭐⭐⭐

How to Hail Taxi

Method 1: Street Hail

1. Stand on sidewalk, look for empty taxi (red light = empty)

2. Wave hand to signal

3. Taxi pulls over

4. Announce destination

Method 2: Taxi App

  • Naver Taxi App (Korean, not recommended for foreigners)
  • Kakao Taxi App (Korean)
  • Uber (available in Seoul; used by English-speakers)
  • Grab (Southeast Asia app, not Seoul)

Method 3: Hotel Concierge

  • Ask hotel staff to call taxi (they know your location)
  • Taxi arrives in 5-10 minutes

How to Use Taxi (English Speakers)

Preparation:

1. Have address written in Korean (or screenshot on phone)

2. Show driver address in Korean

3. Press "Go" button if using app

Communication:

  • Most drivers speak zero English
  • Address + pointing = sufficient
  • Use Google Translate for special requests

Payment:

  • Ask "카드 돼요?" (Card OK?) before getting in
  • Pay at end
  • Tip not necessary (not customary in Seoul)

Taxi Safety

Concern Reality
Safe for women alone? Very safe; many women use taxis solo late night
Robbery/scams? Essentially zero; Seoul is one of safest cities
Driver harassment? Extremely rare; drivers are professional
Meter rigging? Impossible; meter is locked by dispatch

Navigation Apps (The Real MVP)

App 1: Naver Maps (네이버 지도) (Essential)

What It Is: Korean-developed mapping app (superior to Google Maps in Seoul).

Features:

  • Real-time subway/bus routes
  • Walking directions
  • Subway/bus fare calculation
  • Restaurant/cafe search + reviews
  • Live traffic congestion
  • Subway crowding real-time (crucial for rush hour avoidance)

How to Use:

1. Search destination by name or address

2. App shows subway + bus options (fastest highlighted)

3. Shows interchange stations (connections)

4. Estimated arrival time

Language: English fully supported

Download: App Store or Google Play (free)

App 2: KakaoMap (카카오맵) (Backup)

What It Is: Kakao (Korean tech giant) mapping app.

Features:

  • Similar to Naver Map
  • Slightly better restaurant/cafe database
  • Taxi calling (Kakao Taxi built-in)

Language: English supported

Best For: Backup if Naver acts weird; some prefer interface

App 3: Google Maps (Limited Use)

Reality: Google Maps works in Seoul but is inferior to Naver Map.

Problems:

  • Real-time subway/bus data sometimes outdated
  • Station connections sometimes wrong
  • Korean addresses sometimes error

When to Use: English-only speakers with no other option

Recommendation: Download Naver Map first; use Google as backup

Payment Methods (T-Money Card Explained)

What is T-Money?

What It Is: Prepaid transit card, universally accepted in Seoul transit system.

Format: Small plastic card (credit-card sized) with chip

Where Accepted:

  • ✓ All subways
  • ✓ All buses
  • ✓ Taxis (most, but not 100%)
  • ✓ Convenience stores (CU, GS25, CU)
  • ✓ Many vending machines
  • ✓ Some restaurants/cafes

How to Get T-Money

Option 1: Convenience Store

1. Go to CU, GS25, or Emart24 (everywhere, multiple per block)

2. Ask: "티머니 카드 있어요?" (Do you have T-money card?)

3. Say: "충전해주세요" (Charge it, please)

4. State amount: "만원만" (Just 10,000) or "오만원" (50,000)

5. Pay + done

Option 2: Airport

  • Convenience stores in baggage claim area
  • Staff available English-speaking

Option 3: Subway Station

  • Self-service kiosks (English interface)
  • More expensive than convenience store

Cost Breakdown:

  • Card itself: ₩2,500 (one-time, non-refundable)
  • Charge amount: ₩10,000~50,000 (for trips)
  • Reload: Unlimited, anytime

T-Money Benefits

Item Cost without T-Money Cost with T-Money Savings
Single subway trip ₩1,250 ₩1,200 ₩50
5 trips ₩6,250 ₩6,000 ₩250
20 trips ₩25,000 ₩24,000 ₩1,000

Verdict: T-money saves money + convenience; highly recommended

Getting From Incheon Airport to Seoul

Option 1: Subway + Train (Cheapest)

Route: Incheon International Airport (인천공항) → AREX (공항철도, Airport Railroad) → Seoul Station (서울역)

Cost: ₩4,750 (~$3.40 USD)

Time: 60-90 min (including transfers)

How:

1. Follow signs "AREX" or "공항철도" in airport

2. Buy single-use transit card (₩5,000, includes ₩250 refund)

3. Board train toward Seoul/Seocho

4. Ride ~60 min to Seoul Station

5. Transfer to Line 1 for downtown destination

Best For: Budget travelers, not luggage-heavy

Option 2: Airport Limousine Bus (Mid-Range)

Cost: ₩15,000~20,000 (~$11-14 USD)

Time: 60-120 min (depends on destination)

How:

1. Follow signs "Airport Bus" or "공항 버스" in airport

2. Find bus line matching your hotel location

3. Pay at desk or swipe card

4. Bus drops you near hotel (exact address lookup)

Best For: Moderate luggage, knows hotel area

Option 3: Taxi (Convenient but Pricey)

Cost: ₩50,000~70,000 (~$36-50 USD)

Time: 45-90 min (highly variable, traffic)

How:

1. Exit airport to taxi stand

2. Take taxi booth attendant's recommendation

3. Give hotel address (written in Korean)

4. Pay by card

Best For: Luggage-heavy, late arrival, group split

Option 4: Car Rental (Flexibility)

Not Recommended for Most Tourists:

  • Seoul driving is aggressive
  • Parking is nightmare
  • Subway coverage is 99% sufficient
  • Gas + tolls expensive

Consider Only If: Visiting countryside outside Seoul; multi-city road trip

Rush Hour Avoidance

When to Avoid (Peak Hours)

Time Subway Crowding Best Alternative
7:30-9:00 AM Extreme Avoid, work from hotel
9:00-10:00 AM High Use bus instead
5:30-7:30 PM Extreme Delay trip 1-2 hours
7:30-9:00 PM High Still crowded
9:00 PM+ Low-Medium Good time to travel

Peak Hour Realities

Morning Rush (7:30-9 AM):

  • Subway physically packed (shoulder-to-shoulder)
  • Avoid if possible

Evening Rush (5:30-7:30 PM):

  • Even worse than morning
  • Last trains before 11 PM will be crowded

Best Travel Times: 10 AM-5 PM, after 9 PM

Pro Navigation Tips

1. Download Maps Before WiFi Fades

  • Naver Map: Download offline maps (Android: yes; iOS: limited)
  • Google Maps: Download offline map area
  • Backup: Take screenshots of key routes

2. Use Subway Station Transliterations

Subway names written as Romanized Korean:

  • Myeongdong (명동)
  • Gangnam (강남)
  • Jongno 3-ga (종로 3가)

Pro Tip: Learn station names in Korean; staff understand better

3. Ask Locals, They're Helpful

Where to Ask:

  • Subway staff (office windows at stations)
  • Convenience store cashiers
  • Police boxes (파출소; staff speak English)

Language: Show Google Translate with destination; point at screen

4. Respect Directional Signs

Seoul subway has:

  • Clear A/B/C exit designations
  • Exit signs show what's above (restaurant, landmark, address)
  • Follow signs; use them to navigate

5. Understand "Station Transfer"

Definition: Some intersections = walking between stations (same line, different access)

Example: Line 2, Gangnam Station has 11 exits; each serves different areas

Solution: App shows which exit for your destination

Accessibility & Special Needs

Wheelchair/Mobility Assistance

Subway Accessibility:

  • Most stations have elevators (accessibility improving annually)
  • Escalators available; use elevator if hesitant
  • Staff help available (ask at platform)
  • Women-only cars: mobility-friendly

Bus Accessibility:

  • Lower-floor buses available on major routes
  • Ramps for wheelchair access
  • Staff assist if needed

Taxi:

  • Standard taxis fine for wheelchair users
  • Request wheelchair-accessible taxi if needed (Uber app has option)

Hearing/Language Barriers

  • All subway announcements: Korean + English
  • Visual displays: station names shown (Korean + English)
  • Naver Map: Complete English interface

Common Navigation Mistakes

1. Not Having Destination Address

Mistake: "Take me to the shopping district"

Reality: Driver/app needs specific address

Fix: Get Kakao Map PIN or write address in Korean

2. Trusting Google Maps Exclusively

Mistake: Following Google for subway transfer; ends up wrong station

Reality: Naver Map handles Seoul better

Fix: Cross-check with Naver Map

3. Not Checking Rush Hour Times

Mistake: Taking subway 6 PM; it's terrible

Reality: 30-minute trip becomes 45 minutes + stress

Fix: Wait 1-2 hours or use bus/taxi

4. Walking Wrong Direction on Subway

Mistake: Going to "Line 2, Gangnam direction" but actually heading to wrong end

Reality: Line 2 is circular; direction matters (clockwise/counter-clockwise)

Fix: Check app; look at train direction sign before boarding

5. Forgetting to Tap Out of Bus

Mistake: Getting off bus without tapping card

Reality: Charged full fare (no distance calculation benefit)

Fix: Always tap when getting off

FAQ

Q1: Is Subway or Bus Cheaper?

A: Same base fare (₩1,250 per trip), but subway faster.

Value: Subway better value (time is money).

Q2: Can I Use My Phone's Mobile Wallet Instead of T-Money Card?

A: Yes, if your phone supports Korean NFC.

Reality: Apple/Google Pay only work some places; T-money card safer bet.

Pro Tip: Get T-money card; apps add complexity.

Q3: Do Taxis Overcharge Tourists?

A: Meter-locked; impossible to overcharge.

Safety: Taxis are honest; no worries.

Q4: What If I Board Wrong Subway?

A: Get off immediately, transfer correctly.

Transfers: Free within 30 minutes (take new card tap as transfer, not new trip)

Cost: One fare covers entire trip if within transfer window

Q5: Is Public Transport Safe at Night?

A: Very safe. Women-only cars available.

Reality: Millions use subway until close (11 PM) nightly; zero harassment culture

Final Advice

Seoul's transportation system is built for tourists. English signage, apps, and helpful culture make navigation smooth.

Don't overthink it. Get a T-money card, download Naver Map, and move. You'll figure it out.

The system wants you to use it.
