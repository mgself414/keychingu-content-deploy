Namsan (남산) Looping Trail & Night View — Seoul Tower as Your Backdrop

At a Glance

Item Details
Location Namsan (Seoul's center), Jung-gu
Time Needed 1.5~2 hours (leisurely pace)
Starting Point Subway Line 2, Myeongdong Station (명동역) or Chungmuro Station (충무로역)
Best Hours 5 PM~9 PM (sunset + night views)
Difficulty Easy (gentle slopes, many stairs)
Cost None (free, except Tower entry: ₩9,000 extra)
Weekend Crowds Medium~High (sunset time onwards)

Why Namsan Trail & Night View?

"Walk a Full Circle of Seoul While Watching Time Change"

Namsan is the easiest place to experience nature in Seoul's center.

  • Multiple Routes: Cable car, bus, or hiking to the summit — all reach the same N Seoul Tower
  • Shifting Scenery: Daytime (city overview) → Sunset (romanticism) → Night (night views) — three Seouls in one place
  • Free Night Views: Tower entry is paid, but surrounding walking trails offer 100% free night views
  • Solo or Couple: Perfect for solo contemplation or couple dates

Distinct from #002 Free Night View Spot: That one is "watching Seoul from far away"; Namsan is "walking a loop around the Tower while experiencing Seoul from every angle."

Three Ways to Reach Namsan Summit

Choose based on your fitness, time, and budget.

Method 1: Hiking (Myeongdong → Namsan Peak)

Route

Myeongdong Station Exit 8 → Namsan Park (남산공원) → Past Cable Car Station → N Seoul Tower

Distance: ~2 km (leisurely 30~40 min walk)

Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐ (Medium)

Features:

  • Car-free hiking trail
  • Mid-way benches for rest
  • Surrounding landscape best-in-class (city as backdrop to hiking)

Recommended For: Exercise lovers, time-rich travelers

Cost: Free

Tip:

  • Comfortable hiking shoes essential
  • Depart 1 hour before sunset for sunrise + sunset + night views

Method 2: Cable Car (Myeongdong → Cable Car Station → Tower)

Route

Myeongdong Station Exit 8 → Cable Car Station (15 min walk) → Tower (cable car 8 min)

Cable Car Info:

  • Operating Hours: 10 AM~11 PM (seasonal variation)
  • Round-trip Cost: ₩13,000 (standard) / ₩17,000 (all-access)
  • One-way Cost: ₩8,500
  • Boarding Time: 8 min

Difficulty: ⭐ (Very Easy)

Features:

  • Skip steep sections
  • Cable car itself is a scenic viewpoint (city views from inside)
  • Minimal walking
  • Paid

Recommended For: Weak fitness, families with children/elderly, time-limited visitors

Tip:

  • Cable car seats: back seats have better views
  • One-way ticket (₩8,500) option: ride up, hike down for savings
  • Evening cable car 15~30 min wait during peak

Method 3: Bus (Myeongdong → Namsan Direct)

Bus Routes

Bus Departure Duration Cost
02 Myeongdong area 10 min ₩1,250
15 City Hall Square 12 min ₩1,250
05 Jongno 1-ga 15 min ₩1,250

Difficulty: ⭐ (Very Easy)

Features:

  • Most affordable
  • Uphill slope auto-solved
  • Unpredictable wait times (may miss bus)

Recommended For: Budget-conscious, minimum walking desired

Tip:

  • Korean bus card (T-money (티머니)) purchase essential
  • Bus station finding challenging; taxi recommended if unsure
  • Evening peak rush (5~7 PM) — avoid if possible

Experiencing Namsan Trail & Views

Daytime (2 PM~5 PM): City Overview

While Climbing:

  • Myeongdong shoppers gradually disappear below
  • Seoul's buildings enter view progressively
  • Han River (한강), Yeouido (여의도) district visible
  • Gyeongbokgung Palace (경복궁) area (historic quarter) appears

Activity: Leisurely hike, photo stops, bench rests

Sunset (5 PM~7 PM): Sky Transformation & City Lights

This is Namsan's Golden Hour.

Time Sky Recommended Activity
5:00~5:30 Sun dips west, sunset reddening Photo standby
5:30~6:00 Sky shifts orange → purple Best photo time
6:00~6:30 City lights beginning to turn on Day-night boundary
6:30~7:00 Full darkness, city lighting starts Night views begin

Photo Tip:

  • 5:45~6:15 = Golden Time (unlit sky + city lights = perfect combination)
  • Tripod useful for better sunset shots
  • Phone landscape mode excellent for capturing city-scape

Night (7 PM~10 PM): Full Night Views

As darkness deepens:

  • Every city light turns on
  • Han River lighting reflects beautifully
  • Gyeongbokgung, Gwanghwamun (광화문), Deoksugung (덕수궁) night lighting
  • Yeouido high-rise building lighting

Activity:

  • Slow-walk Namsan's perimeter trails
  • 360-degree views by changing angles
  • Bench rest while observing

2~4 PM ⭐⭐ Quiet but heavy, no sunset
4~6 PM ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Best (sunset + night views)
6~8 PM ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Night views optimal
8 PM+ ⭐⭐⭐ Good night views, crowded

Strongly Recommended: 4 PM departure (captures sunset timing perfectly).

3. Photo Tips

Technique Tip
Golden Hour Usage 5:45~6:15 (sky color + city lights combo)
Angle Changes Tower foreground, to the side, full shot — vary angles
Foreground Hand, shoulder, tree branch as foreground = depth
Night Photography Use phone night mode, stabilize (or tripod)
DSLR users Raise ISO high, adjust shutter speed

4. Tower Entry vs Surrounding Trail

N Seoul Tower Entry (Paid)

Entry Cost: ₩9,000~13,000 (varies by floor)

See:

  • 360-degree rotating views
  • Indoor lighting (pleasant)
  • Cafe/dining available

Downside: Price, queues (evening 30 min~1 hour)

Outside Trail (Free)

See:

  • Tower as backdrop
  • Wind + cool air feeling
  • 360-degree angles freedom

Advantage: Free, no time limits

Recommendation: First visit or time-rich = tower entry. Tight budget/time = outside trails only.

Safety Checklist

Night Hiking Safety

  • [ ] Adequate lighting (phone + headlamp preferable)
  • [ ] Comfortable shoes (slips risk)
  • [ ] Insect repellent (summer)
  • [ ] Warm clothing (winter/late evening)
  • [ ] Prefer going with a companion

Avoid

  • Bad weather (fog, high wind)
  • Solo after 10 PM (less crowded = less safe)
  • Drunk state (stumbling risk on stairs)
  • Heels (very slippery)

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1: Namsan vs #002 Free Night View Spot — Which Is Better?

A: Different experiences.

Item Namsan Free Spot
Experience Walk loop, experience Tower View from far, observe
Duration 1~2 hours (active) 20~30 min (passive)
Activity Level High Low
When Short on Time No Yes

Advice: Time available = Namsan. Tight time = Free Spot.

Q2: Cable Car Worth It?

A: No. Hiking is free and equally rewarding.

Cable car for:

  • Weak fitness
  • Families with young children
  • Time-limited

Q3: Solo Safe at Night?

A: Yes, very crowded and safe.

Caution:

  • By 8 PM crowds thin slightly
  • After 10 PM = fewer people (avoid solo)
  • Valuables: always confirm

Q4: Do Tower Photos Need Tower Entry?

A: No. Outside trails = stunning Tower backdrop photos.

Final Advice

Namsan is a place where each visitor has different experience.

Same path:

  • Some go romantic (couples)
  • Some go meditative (solo)
  • Some go active (hikers)
  • Some go convenient (cable car)

Experience Namsan your way.

At Seoul's highest point, spend time alone. The city below is yours to observe.
