Seoul Noraebang Guide for Foreign Travelers

🎤 What Is a Noraebang (노래방)?

"In Korea, Noraebang isn't just a hobby — it's a way of life."

After company dinners, on dates, as a stress outlet, to kill time with friends on a late night... if you're Korean, you go to a Noraebang (노래방). Can't sing? Doesn't matter. Neither can most Koreans. This isn't a place for good singers — it's a place for everyone. You can even go alone and it's totally normal. It might feel a little unfamiliar the first time, but you'll get the hang of it fast. Noraebang is one of the best ways to truly experience Korean culture.

A quick explainer: Noraebang literally means "singing room" — it's a private karaoke room where you and your friends (or just you!) sing your hearts out in your own space, with no audience of strangers. Very different from Western-style karaoke bars.

🎧 The 3 Types of Noraebang

1️⃣ Coin Noraebang (코인노래방)

Best for going solo or with 2–3 friends for a quick sing

Item Details
Price ₩5,000–10,000 (about 10–25 songs)
Time Pay per song (coins or card)
Space Small booth (fits 1–2 people)
Vibe Spontaneous, budget-friendly, easy in and out
Atmosphere Super casual — totally fine to go solo

How it works — You'll find these everywhere near subway stations, in alleyways, next to convenience stores. Pay with coins or a transit card, step into your booth, search for a song, and start singing. Popular chains like Seven Star (세븐스타), Dream Singer (드림싱어), I GO, and Coin Singer have modern machines and great sound systems.

2️⃣ Standard Noraebang (일반노래방)

Best for groups (3+ people) who want to stay a while

Item Details
Price ₩10,000–20,000/hour (peak-time surcharges apply)
Time Hourly rate (daytime discounts available)
Space Small, medium, or large rooms — your pick
Vibe Relaxed, comfortable, perfect for friend groups
Atmosphere Laid-back, family- and friend-friendly

How it works — These cluster around university areas and busy shopping streets. You pick your room size, and many places let you order snacks and drinks. Daytime hours (10am–3pm) are usually discounted — great if you're watching your budget.

3️⃣ Luxury / Drink Noraebang (럭셔리/술 노래방)

For special nights, anniversaries, or full VIP treatment

Item Details
Price ₩30,000–50,000+/hour
Time Minimum 1 hour
Space Large private room, hotel-level setup
Vibe Premium sound, drinks and snacks available
Atmosphere Luxury, exclusive, very private

How it works — Concentrated in premium areas like Gangnam (강남) and Hongdae (홍대). Drinks and food are served, and you get high-end machines and mood lighting. Perfect for company outings, birthday parties, or any special occasion.

📋 Step-by-Step Noraebang Guide

Step 1️⃣ Walk In and Pick Your Room

For standard noraebang:

1. Open the door — a staff member will be there.

2. They'll ask "몇 분이에요?" (How many people?). Tell them your group size.

3. Choose your room size. (Small = 2–3 people, Medium = 4–5, Large = 6+)

4. Get your room number and head to your room.

5. Inside you'll find a remote and microphones.

For coin noraebang:

  • Pay at the machine yourself.
  • Open the booth door and walk in.

Step 2️⃣ How to Work the Machine

🎹 Standard Noraebang (Remote Control)

Basic buttons:

  • Song search: Search by artist name, song title, or song number
  • Language selection: Look for "ENG" or a globe icon at the top of the remote — you can switch to English, Chinese, or Japanese search
  • Queue a song: Select a song and tap "예약" (reserve) to add it to the lineup
  • Volume control: +/- buttons (Korean noraebangs default to loud — feel free to turn it down)
  • Echo: Adds reverb to your mic — crank it up as a beginner and your voice will sound way better
  • Key adjustment: Raise or lower the pitch to match your vocal range
  • Tempo: Speed the song up or slow it down

📱 Coin Noraebang (Tablet or Touch Screen)

Most modern coin noraebangs use a touchscreen tablet for searching. Major chain locations support English, Chinese, and Japanese interfaces — just tap the language option at the top.

Step 3️⃣ Finding Songs

The two main noraebang machine brands in Korea: TJ Media (TJ미디어) and KY (금영) — Gold Star.

📍 Song Number Ranges (approximate)

Songs are organized by number. It varies by machine and update, but the general pattern is:

  • K-POP (Korean): 100,000–700,000 range
  • English (Pop, R&B): Around 70,000–80,000 range
  • Japanese songs: Around 80,000–90,000 range
  • Chinese songs: Around 90,000 range

Searching:

1. If you know the title, try typing it in English (e.g., "DYNAMITE")

2. Artist names work too (e.g., "BTS")

3. If it doesn't come up, it might be on a different machine brand or not yet loaded for new releases

4. You can enter the song number directly (a Korean friend can help you find it)

Tips for finding songs as a foreigner:

  • Tap the "ENGLISH" button on the tablet interface to browse the English song category
  • Check the latest chart tab — popular songs are grouped there
  • If you're not sure what to pick, hit the "추천곡" (recommended songs) tab — these are the ones Koreans sing most

Step 4️⃣ Adjusting Your Sound

Press the mute button on the mic to play music only — handy when you want to listen while your friend sings.

  • Echo: Higher = more reverb. Try +2–3 as a beginner for that polished sound.
  • Key: If the pitch is too high, lower it (-2 to -3). Too low? Raise it (+2 to +3).
  • Volume: Some machines let you adjust the track volume and mic volume separately.

Step 5️⃣ Instruments (Optional)

Most standard noraebangs have small tambourines (탬버린) and maracas (마라카스) in the room — grab one and rock out to the beat. Guaranteed to make things more fun.

  • Tambourine: Shake it rhythmically — "shish shish shish"
  • Maracas: Shake along to the beat
  • Castanets: Perfect for Spanish-style or Latin-beat songs

🏪 Best Noraebang Areas & Chains in Seoul

Area Guide

🌃 Hongdae (홍대)

  • Coin noraebang central
  • Young crowd, tons of aesthetically designed coin rooms
  • Cheap prices, super accessible
  • Also has: Luxe noraebangs (premium options) for when you want to splurge

🏙️ Gangnam (강남)

  • Luxury/drink noraebang territory
  • Upscale vibe throughout
  • Drinks and food menus on point
  • Pricier (₩30,000–50,000/hour)

🎓 University Areas (Hongik, Ewha, Yonsei)

  • Mix of standard and coin noraebangs
  • Student-friendly pricing
  • Late-night discounts galore (after midnight)

🚇 Jongno (종로)

  • All types mixed together
  • Great subway access
  • Visitor-friendly area

🎬 Special Features

Recording options:

  • Modern coin noraebang chains have built-in recording features
  • Some luxury noraebangs offer HD camera video recording
  • You can also just record yourself on your phone (music plays as background)

Idea: Record your best song and share it with your friends. Instant core memory.

🎤 Final Tips

1. Most Koreans can't sing either — seriously. Don't stress about sounding perfect. Just have fun.

2. Noraebang is a cultural experience — treat it like a tourism activity and enjoy every second.

3. English songs are everywhere — if Korean songs feel intimidating, start with English or international hits.

4. Prices are fixed — no haggling.

5. Book longer, pay less per hour — booking 5 hours is cheaper per hour than booking 1 hour at a time.

6. Start the mic volume high — sing a bit, then adjust to what feels right.

7. Going solo is 100% normal — many Koreans go alone to decompress after a long day. You're in good company.

The Real Magic of Seoul Noraebangs

A Noraebang isn't just a leisure spot. It's where K-POP passion, Korean emotions, shared memories, and the culture of letting go all collide in one room.

You might miss some lyrics. Your pitch might be off. But that's exactly the point. It's not about being perfect — it's about laughing and having a great time together.

One of the most Korean things you can do in Korea. If you've been to a noraebang, you're already one of us.
