Three Mistakes International Visitors Most Often Make

1. Believing "Duty-free = Always Cheapest"

Duty-free is genuinely cheaper for global luxury brands (Chanel, Dior, etc.). But for K-Beauty, K-Food, and character merchandise, city stores (Olive Young / 올리브영, Daiso / 다이소, Line Friends / 라인프렌즈) are equally cheap or cheaper — and have far more variety.

2. Buying All Traditional Crafts at Insadong (인사동)

Insadong (인사동) wins on English-friendliness and curation, but prices run 1.5–2× the regular markets. The same embroidered coin purse (자수 손지갑) costs ₩8,000 on the 1F of Gwangjang Market (광장시장) and ₩15,000–20,000 in Insadong (인사동).

3. Saving All K-Beauty for the Airport Duty-Free

The airport duty-free shop fills up in the last 1–2 hours before departure, and popular SKUs run out. Buy at city duty-free shops (Lotte / 롯데, Shinsegae / 신세계) in advance and pick up at the airport pickup desk for safety.

① Olive Young Myeongdong Town (올리브영 명동타운점) — K-Beauty's #1 Foreigner Destination

Location: 53 Myeongdong-gil, Jung-gu, Seoul — Subway Line 4, Myeongdong Station (명동역) Exit 6, 3-min walk

Why here:

  • 2025 cumulative foreign-visitor purchases passed ₩1 trillion, +53% YoY
  • 8 Olive Young (올리브영) locations in Myeongdong (명동) alone; the Town store is 95% foreign sales with 188 nationalities visiting
  • K-Beauty cleansing, skincare, masks, and beauty devices all under one roof

Must-buy categories (as of 2026-04):

  • Sheet masks: Mediheal (메디힐), Innisfree (이니스프리), Round Lab (라운드랩) — ₩1,000–3,000 each
  • Sunscreen / cushion: Round Lab (라운드랩), Anua (아누아), COSRX (코스알엑스) — ₩15,000–30,000
  • Cleansing oil: Banila Co. (바닐라코), Avène, Ko-Logy (콜로지) — ₩15,000–25,000
  • Beauty devices: Medicube (메디큐브), Celliturn (셀리턴) — ₩100,000–300,000 (cheaper at duty-free)

Tips:

  • Multi-language staff (EN / JP / CN) at Myeongdong Town store
  • Global membership signup → 5–10% extra discount
  • Tax refund counter inside store for single receipts ₩30,000+

② Daiso Myeongdong / Hongdae (다이소 명동·홍대) — K-General Goods Value Champion

Locations:

  • Myeongdong-yeok store (다이소 명동역점): Myeongdong Station (명동역) Exit 6, directly connected (12-floor flagship)
  • Hongdae store (다이소 홍대점): Hongik University Station (홍대입구역) Exit 9, 5-min walk

Why here:

  • Daiso (다이소) overseas card payment growth: 2022 +300% → 2023 +130% → 2024 +50% → 2025 +60%
  • Steady stream of foreign visitors — #1 K-general-goods destination
  • Price points of ₩1,000–5,000, so a bag full of gifts stays under ₩50,000

Must-buy categories:

  • Character socks / keyrings: ₩1,000–3,000
  • Mini hanbok (한복) figures, Taegeuk (태극) merchandise: ₩2,000–5,000
  • Korean snacks (Ppeong-twigi / 뻥튀기, Saeu-kkang / 새우깡, mini Banana Milk / 바나나우유): ₩1,000–3,000
  • Daiso-line K-Beauty mini sizes: ₩3,000–5,000
  • Daiso Hanji (한지) notebooks, calligraphy pens: ₩2,000–5,000

Tips:

  • The Myeongdong-yeok flagship (12 floors) is the largest and most foreigner-friendly
  • Tax refund available for receipts ₩30,000+ (ask staff)

③ Gwangjang Market (광장시장) + Olyoung Yanghaeng (올영양행) — Tradition Meets K-Beauty

Location: 88 Changgyeonggung-ro, Jongno-gu, Seoul — Subway Line 1, Jongno 5-ga Station (종로5가역) Exit 8, 2-min walk

Why here:

  • Gwangjang Market (광장시장) is itself the #1 foreigner food destination (mayak gimbap / 마약김밥, bindaetteok / 빈대떡, yukhoe / 육회)
  • The newly opened Olyoung Yanghaeng (올영양행) in 2026 — a 1960s-shop reinterpretation + K-Beauty experience format
  • Tradition + K-Beauty in one alley = the home of the "K-Dailycation" trend

Must-buy categories:

  • 1F food alley: 8 mayak gimbap (마약김밥) ₩5,000, 1 bindaetteok (빈대떡) ₩6,000–8,000 (try on site, take vacuum-packed)
  • 1F hanbok (한복) / linen lane: embroidered coin purses (자수 손지갑) ₩8,000–15,000, bojagi wrapping cloths (보자기) ₩5,000–12,000
  • 2F vintage arcade: pattern dresses ₩10,000–40,000 (separate guide #50)
  • Olyoung Yanghaeng (올영양행): limited-edition K-Beauty + market-collab merchandise

Tips:

  • Weekday morning 10–12 is least crowded and freshest
  • 1F food alley accepts cash preferentially
  • Tax refund only at Olyoung Yanghaeng (올영양행) and select fixed shops — not at street stalls

④ Insadong Ssamziegil (인사동 쌈지길) + Insadong (인사동) Street — Traditional Crafts · Art

Location: 44 Insadong-gil, Jongno-gu, Seoul — Subway Line 3, Anguk Station (안국역) Exit 6, 5-min walk

Why here:

  • #1 in foreigner-friendliness (100% English signage, multi-language)
  • Curated traditional craft hub — embroidery, ceramics, hanji (한지), brushes, traditional tea
  • Ssamziegil (쌈지길) is a 4-floor spiral mall + rooftop garden = stay-and-shop layout

Must-buy categories:

  • Embroidered coin purses (자수 손지갑) / bojagi (보자기): ₩15,000–30,000 (1.5–2× Gwangjang but better curation)
  • Hanji (한지) crafts (bookmarks, lanterns): ₩5,000–50,000
  • Ceramic cups / plates: ₩20,000–150,000
  • Traditional tea + tea-set: ₩30,000–200,000
  • Calligraphy artworks: ₩10,000–100,000

Tips:

  • Insadong (인사동) = curation + English / Gwangjang (광장) = value + local
  • Coffee break at Ssamziegil rooftop café recommended
  • Walk to Anguk → Insadong → Bukchon Hanok Village (북촌 한옥마을) within 1 km

⑤ Namdaemun Market (남대문시장) — Practical · Ginseng · Wholesale

Location: Namdaemun-sijang 4-gil, Jung-gu, Seoul — Subway Line 4, Hoehyeon Station (회현역) Exit 5, 1-min walk

Why here:

  • 600-year-old Korea's largest mixed market
  • Korean ginseng (인삼) / red ginseng (홍삼) at near-wholesale prices
  • High wholesale share — bedding, hanbok, kids' wear, stationery, watches

Must-buy categories:

  • Cheong Kwan Jang (정관장) 6-year red ginseng: 5–15% cheaper than duty-free (only at official-distributor stalls)
  • Korean socks (10 pairs / ₩10,000): character + traditional patterns
  • Wholesale dishes / tableware: ₩2,000–10,000 each
  • Eyewear frames (wholesale): about half of Myeongdong (명동)

Tips:

  • The midnight wholesale window (00:00–04:00) is not foreigner-friendly
  • Hours: 08:00–17:00 (retail), 23:00–04:00 (wholesale)
  • Tax refund available at limited shops only — Cheong Kwan Jang (정관장) and a few hanbok (한복) stores
  • English signage weaker than Insadong (인사동) and Myeongdong (명동)

⑥ Lotte / Shinsegae City Duty-Free (시내면세점) — Global Luxury + Premium K-Beauty

Locations:

  • Lotte Duty-Free Myeongdong (롯데면세점 명동본점): Euljiro 1-ga Station (을지로입구역) Exit 8, directly connected (Lotte Department Store 9F–12F)
  • Shinsegae Duty-Free Myeongdong (신세계면세점 명동점): Myeongdong Station (명동역) Exit 10 (Shinsegae Department Store 8F–12F)

Why here:

  • 10–30% cheaper than retail for global luxury (Chanel, Dior, Gucci)
  • Premium K-Beauty devices (Medicube / 메디큐브, Celliturn / 셀리턴) and ginseng (Cheong Kwan Jang / 정관장) bundled with discounts
  • Passport + departure flight registered → duty-free processed → pick up at airport delivery desk

Must-buy categories:

  • Luxury cosmetics (Lancôme, SK-II): –15–25% vs retail
  • Cheong Kwan Jang (정관장) red ginseng concentrate / sliced: duty-free price + extra membership discount
  • Premium K-Beauty devices: Medicube AGE-R, Celliturn LED Mask
  • Fragrance: full global brand lineup

Tips:

  • Foreign nationals only, departing within 60 days
  • At checkout you must provide passport + outbound flight info
  • Duty-free allowance varies by destination: USA $800 / Japan ¥200,000 etc.
  • Pickup desks: Incheon Airport (인천공항) T1 near Gate 14, T2 near Gate 28

FAQs from International Visitors

Q1. How does Tax Refund work?

  • Single receipt ₩30,000+, foreign passport
  • Instant Refund at the store, or Airport Refund before security
  • Refund amount: about 9–10% (VAT + partial excise tax)
  • Olive Young (올리브영) Myeongdong (명동), Daiso (다이소), and duty-free shops have instant-refund counters

Q2. How do I receive duty-free items?

  • City duty-free purchase → pick up at Incheon Airport (인천공항) delivery desk
  • T1 near Gate 14 / T2 near Gate 28
  • Arrive at the airport 3 hours before departure to allow time

Q3. Are foreign credit cards accepted?

  • Myeongdong (명동), Insadong (인사동), duty-free, department stores, Olive Young (올리브영), Daiso (다이소): VISA / Mastercard / Amex all OK
  • Gwangjang (광장) / Namdaemun (남대문) market stalls: cash preferred
  • Some stores reject foreign cards — bring some ₩100,000 + ₩50,000 bills as backup

Q4. What's the limit for taking K-Beauty home?

  • USA: cosmetic liquids generally fine (carry-on 100ml rule)
  • Japan: under 24 units per item
  • China: simple cosmetics within 5,000 RMB total
  • 100ml liquid rule applies to carry-on only — checked baggage is fine

Q5. Airport duty-free vs City duty-free, which is better?

  • City: more variety, time flexibility, delivery-desk system
  • Airport: short on time / small purchases / candies, cookies for last-minute add-ons
  • Recommendation: 70% city, 30% airport

Q6. What truly Korean gifts can I buy only here?

  • Naju Pear / Hallabong jam (나주배 / 한라봉 잼): Nonghyup (농협) / E-Mart (이마트) / airport duty-free
  • Spoon-and-chopstick sets (수저 세트) in brass / silver: Insadong (인사동) / Gwangjang (광장)
  • Embroidered coin purses · bojagi (보자기): Gwangjang (광장) / Insadong (인사동)
  • Hanji (한지) crafts: Insadong (인사동)
  • Korean dried seaweed gift boxes (조미김 박스): duty-free / E-Mart (이마트)
  • Ginseng / red ginseng: Cheong Kwan Jang (정관장, duty-free recommended)
  • K-Pop albums (with photo cards): SM Town (SM타운) / HYBE

Q7. What Korean micro-souvenirs are worth buying at Daiso (다이소)?

  • Mini hanbok (한복) doll: ₩2,000
  • Mini Taegeukgi (태극기) pin badge: ₩1,000
  • Hangeul (한글) calligraphy notebook: ₩3,000
  • Traditional-pattern hand mirror: ₩2,000–3,000
  • Saekdong (색동) striped socks: ₩1,000–2,000

Q8. What are the most regretted foreigner shopping mistakes?

1. Buying everything at Insadong (인사동) (1.5–2× pricier)

2. Saving all purchases for the airport (last-minute time crunch)

3. Putting K-Pop light sticks (응원봉) in checked baggage → loss

4. Losing tax-refund receipts → 9–10% refund forfeited

5. Buying so much K-Beauty that baggage exceeds the limit

Seasonal Notes

Spring (March – May)

  • Most recommended: K-Beauty new launches + spring-limited packaging
  • On heavy fine-dust days, masks recommended at markets

Summer (June – August)

  • Airport / city duty-free peak season — pickup desk lines longer
  • Ramen / snacks travel fine in summer, but chocolate items melt — caution

Autumn (September – November)

  • Hanbok (한복) / embroidery autumn lineup — Insadong (인사동) recommended
  • Around Chuseok (추석, early October) some shops close — verify ahead

Winter (December – February)

  • Year-end duty-free discounts peak (mid-December to year-end)
  • Gwangjang (광장) cold so focus on indoor digging + food
  • Limited-edition holiday merchandise (Christmas + New Year)
