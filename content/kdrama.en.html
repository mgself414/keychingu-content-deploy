Walk the Scenes Yourself: Seoul K-Drama Filming Spots

A Self-Tour Guide for Drama Fans

> What if you could stand in the exact spot where your favorite scene was filmed?

> Follow the real filming locations of Seoul's most iconic dramas — the ultimate itinerary for any K-drama fan

📺 Drama-by-Drama Location Guide

1. Queen of Tears (눈물의 여왕, 2024) — A Fantastical Mansion of Love and Betrayal

Drama overview: A psychological romance about a wealthy wife, a husband's suspected infidelity, and a search for real love — with plenty of surprises along the way (minimal spoilers — trust us, go in fresh)

📍 Key Filming Locations

Wooriyet Stone Museum (우리옛돌박물관) — Seongbuk-gu, Seoul

  • Role: The Queen Group Chairman's mansion (scenes filmed here with the lead cast)
  • Address: 66 Daesagwan-ro 13-gil, Seongbuk-gu, Seoul
  • Subway: Line 4, Hansung University Station (한성대입구역) → taxi or 15-min walk
  • Highlights: A stunning blend of traditional Korean architecture and modern design that creates the drama's luxurious atmosphere
  • Visit tip: Currently operating as a museum — confirm opening hours on Naver Maps before visiting (a filming location visit requires advance check)
  • How to get that shot: Use the changing sunlight in the garden. The backlighting around 3–4pm is the most dramatic

Walkerhill Hotel Aston House (워커힐 호텔 애스톤 하우스) — Gwangjin-gu, Seoul

  • Role: Wedding ceremony scenes (Episode 4)
  • Address: 177 Walkerhill-ro, Gwangjin-gu, Seoul
  • Subway: Line 5, Gwangnaru Station (광나루역) → 5-min taxi
  • Highlights: An elegant banquet hall ideal for the drama's dreamy backlit scenes
  • Nearby dining: Multiple dining options within Walkerhill Hotel
  • How to get that shot: Stand by a window with the Seoul skyline behind you. Frame yourself on one side using the rule of thirds to capture that emotional depth

Ilwol Arboretum (일월수목원) — Suwon, Gyeonggi-do

  • Role: A forest scene from the early episodes
  • Address: 61 Ilwol-ro, Jangan-gu, Suwon, Gyeonggi-do
  • Subway: Line 1, Suwon Station (수원역) → 20-min taxi
  • Highlights: Dense natural forest that creates dramatic shadow and light — a setting that feels straight out of a film
  • Visit tip: Looks completely different by season — cherry blossoms in spring, fall foliage in autumn are both stunning
  • How to get that shot: Use the contrast of light and shadow between the trees. Try Portrait Mode on your phone for a soft bokeh background

Geonji Village, Chungju (충주 건지마을) — Chungju, North Chungcheong Province

  • Role: Early rural episode filming location
  • Address: Geonji Village, Dongnyang-myeon, Chungju, Chungcheong Province
  • Transit: Seoul ↔ Chungju Mugunghwa train, approximately 1 hour 50 min
  • Highlights: Classic Korean countryside scenery, used to create contrast with the urban main storyline
  • How to get that shot: Shoot from a low angle looking down the country road with your subject in the foreground. Golden hour (just before sunset) gives it that emotional quality

2. Lovely Runner (선재 업고 튀어, 2024) — A Time-Slip Romance, Meeting Across Decades

Drama overview: An extraordinary love story that unfolds across 2024 and 2008, with a main character jumping between timelines (keeping it spoiler-light!)

📍 Key Filming Locations

Seongbuk Stream Stepping Stone Bridge (성북천 여울다리) — Seongbuk-gu, Seoul

  • Role: The bridge where the time-slip begins — the central location for all timeline transitions in the drama
  • Address: Around Seongbuk-dong 2-ga, Seongbuk-gu, Seoul
  • Subway: Line 4, Sungshin Women's University Station (성신여대역), Exit 1 → 5-min walk
  • Highlights: A peaceful walking path with a quiet atmosphere — now a pilgrimage spot for drama fans
  • Nearby cafes: Cafe street near Sungshin Women's University Station (Mont de and others)
  • Visit tip: Arrive early morning to have the bridge mostly to yourself
  • How to get that shot: Stand at the railing and shoot straight on. Vertical/portrait mode captures both the water and trees behind you perfectly

Dongdaemun Design Plaza (DDP, 동대문 디자인 플라자) — Jung-gu, Seoul

  • Role: A concert venue in the 2024 timeline — where the lead character watches her favorite idol perform
  • Address: 45 Euljiro, Jung-gu, Seoul
  • Subway: Line 2, Dongdaemun Station (동대문역) / Dongdaemun History & Culture Park Station (동대문역사문화공원역)
  • Highlights: Futuristic architecture that represents the modern Seoul of 2024
  • Entry: Indoor exhibitions/events have separate admission; outdoor photography is free
  • Visit tip: The nighttime view is unreal (6–8pm is peak)
  • How to get that shot: Use a wide angle to capture DDP's flowing curves. Include the sky to emphasize the building's grandeur

Seoul Jungang High School (서울 중앙고등학교) — Gwangjin-gu, Seoul

  • Role: School scenes set in 2008
  • Address: 408 Achasan-ro, Gwangjin-gu, Seoul
  • Subway: Line 7, Gwangnaru Station (광나루역) → 10-min walk
  • Highlights: Brings back that nostalgic school-days feeling
  • Visit tip: It's an active school — photos from outside only (near the fence)
  • How to get that shot: Pose near the school gate or wall for a classic drama-style scene

Suwon Haenggung-dong (수원 행궁동) — Suwon, Gyeonggi-do

  • Role: The street in front of where the lead character lives, and the alley where Café Mont de is located — the everyday backdrop for multiple key characters
  • Address: Around 127 Haenggung-ro, Paldal-gu, Suwon, Gyeonggi-do
  • Subway: Line 1, Suwon Station (수원역) → 10-min taxi or bus
  • Highlights: A charming mix of traditional hanok houses and modern cafes
  • Nearby cafes: Mont de (the house from the drama), Haenggung-dong cafe street
  • Visit tip: Afternoon sunlight (2–4pm) hits the alleyways just right
  • How to get that shot: Shoot from close up down the narrow alley with your subject facing the camera. The hanok behind naturally creates a beautiful bokeh effect

3. Goblin (도깨비, 2016, An Evergreen Classic) — A Love Story That Spans Eternity

Drama overview: A fantasy romance following a 900-year-old goblin (dokkaebi, 도깨비), a grim reaper (저승사자), and the women who change their fates forever (one of the all-time greats — go in with as little knowledge as possible)

📍 Key Filming Locations

Gangreung Youngjin Beach (강릉 영진해변) — Gangreung, Gangwon Province

  • Role: The fate-filled beach where the female lead first summons the Goblin — the heart of the drama's central encounter
  • Address: Around Jeongdongjin-ri, Gangdong-myeon, Gangreung, Gangwon
  • Transit: Seoul Cheongnyangni Station (청량리역) → Gangreung Station (강릉역) by Mugunghwa train, approx. 3 hours 30 min; then taxi 20 min from Gangreung Station
  • Highlights: The vast East Sea (동해) here feels eternal — exactly what the drama was going for. A true pilgrimage site for Goblin fans
  • Nearby: Cafes and restaurants near Jeongdongjin Station (정동진역)
  • Visit tip: Go at sunrise (5–6am) or sunset (6–7pm) to truly recreate the drama's mystical atmosphere
  • How to get that shot: Use backlighting from the sea. A silhouette against the horizon captures all the mystery and longing of the drama

Gangreung Gyeongpodae Pavilion (강릉 경포대) — Gangreung, Gangwon Province

  • Role: Where the Goblin and the Grim Reaper take their walks together — their friendship's backdrop
  • Address: Yeongdong area, Gangreung, Gangwon
  • Transit: 15-min taxi from Gangreung Station
  • Highlights: A 600-year-old pavilion that is a masterpiece of Korean traditional architecture — and a famous photo spot in its own right
  • Fun fact: Drama fans turned this into a major tourism destination after the show aired
  • Visit tip: Spring cherry blossoms (April) or autumn foliage (October) — both incredible
  • How to get that shot: Use the pavilion's pillars and railings as a natural frame. Place the mountains and lake in the background using the rule of thirds

Gangreung Wolhwa Street (강릉 월화거리) — Gangreung, Gangwon Province

  • Role: Where the Goblin and the female lead go on dates — a retro street of cafes and shops built along a former railway
  • Address: Around Ponam-dong, Gangreung (former railway line)
  • Highlights: Packed with retro-aesthetic cafes — every corner is a photo opportunity
  • Nearby: 30–40 cafes lining the street
  • Visit tip: Late afternoon (3–4pm) when the cafe lights start coming on is the most atmospheric
  • How to get that shot: Wide angle from a cafe entrance with the whole street behind you. Or shoot a reflection of yourself in a cafe window for that cinematic quality

Gangreung Jungang Market (강릉 중앙시장) — Gangreung, Gangwon Province

  • Role: The everyday market where the Goblin and the female lead shop together
  • Address: Around Jungheon, Gangreung
  • Highlights: The full energy of a real Korean traditional market — plus authentic local street food
  • Must-try: Squid soup, jjim (steamed dishes), sanghae bibimbap (~₩10,000–15,000)
  • Visit tip: Arrive early (8–10am) to catch the market at its most lively

Incheon Baedari Used Book Alley (인천 배다리 헌책방 골목) — Jung-gu, Incheon

  • Role: A brief but pivotal Goblin filming location — a destined meeting place surrounded by pencils and books
  • Address: Gumgok-dong Baedari Used Book Alley, Jung-gu, Incheon
  • Subway: Line 1, Incheon Station (인천역) → 15-min walk
  • Highlights: The atmospheric backdrop for an intuition-driven moment in the story, in front of Hanmi Bookstore (한미서점)
  • Visit tip: Morning light is warmest (10am–12pm)
  • How to get that shot: Capture the alley's depth by shooting straight down it. Stack books in the foreground and let the background blur — instant film effect

4. Itaewon Class (이태원 클라쓰, 2020, Newly Trending Again) — Revenge and Love in the Neon Night

Drama overview: A satisfying revenge story and romance set against the backdrop of Itaewon's (이태원) bar and restaurant scene (a crowd-pleaser — safe to go in knowing as little as possible)

📍 Key Filming Locations

Nokseongpyeong Station Overpass (녹사평역 육교) — Yongsan-gu, Seoul

  • Role: The symbolic stage of the drama — the overpass where the male lead has some of his most important moments (all the iconic scenes)
  • Address: Around Nokseongpyeong-ro, Yongsan-gu, Seoul
  • Subway: Line 6, Nokseongpyeong Station (녹사평역), Exit 3 — right there
  • Highlights: One of the best spots to look out over all of Itaewon — a must-do for any fan of this drama
  • Nearby: Pojangmacha (street food tent bars) around the station
  • Visit tip: Night view from 7–9pm is breathtaking
  • How to get that shot: Lean on the railing and shoot straight on. Let the Itaewon street behind you go soft for that emotional, character-driven look

Namsan Baekbeom Square (남산 백범광장) — Yongsan-gu, Seoul

  • Role: The walking path where the male lead goes every day — his space for reflection and resolve
  • Address: Namsan area, Yongsan-gu, Seoul
  • Subway: Line 3, Chungmuro Station (충무로역) or Line 4, Myeongdong Station (명동역)
  • Highlights: Connected to the Seoul City Wall trail — peaceful outdoor space with completely different scenery each season
  • Visit tip: Early morning (6–8am) or golden hour (5–7pm) for the most dramatic single beam of light
  • How to get that shot: Low angle looking up the steps or path, with the Seoul skyline in the background

Itaewon Street (Nokseongpyeong area, 이태원 거리) — Yongsan-gu, Seoul

  • Role: The main stage of Itaewon Class — the full bar and restaurant district
  • Address: Along Itaewon-ro within 1.5km of Nokseongpyeong Station
  • Subway: Line 6, Nokseongpyeong Station (녹사평역)
  • Highlights: Seoul's most multicultural neighborhood — food, fashion, and nightlife from around the world
  • Nearby: Bars, pubs, Korean restaurants, cafes — hundreds of options
  • Visit tip: Streets light up after 7pm. Friday through Sunday evenings have the best energy
  • How to get that shot: Capture the neon signs alongside the flow of the crowd for a dynamic, cinematic feel

5. Crash Landing on You (사랑의 불시착, 2019, A Masterpiece Worth Revisiting) — Fate Across a Border

Drama overview: A romance between a chaebol heiress who accidentally crash-lands in North Korea and a North Korean officer — a timeless love story (a true classic; minimal plot details below)

📍 Key Filming Locations (Seoul scenes)

Gangnam Luxury Shopping District (서울 강남 고급 매장가) — Gangnam-gu, Seoul

  • Role: The female lead's glamorous everyday life — high-end shopping and corporate scenes
  • Address: Apgujeong-ro and Nonhyeon-ro, Gangnam-gu (Sinsa-dong Garosu-gil, near Gangnam Station)
  • Subway: Line 2, Gangnam Station (강남역); Sinsa Station (신사역)
  • Highlights: Seoul's most luxurious shopping corridor — the embodiment of the character's wealthy lifestyle
  • Recommended cafes: Apgujeong Rodeo Street cafes
  • Visit tip: Afternoon (2–4pm) when store window lighting is sharpest
  • How to get that shot: Pose at a luxury boutique entrance in a sleek, confident stance. Frame the shot around the English/luxury signage for an editorial look

Seoul Arts Center (서울 예술의전당) — Seocho-gu, Seoul

  • Role: Corporate building and backdrop for the female lead's business scenes
  • Address: 2406 Nambusunhwan-ro, Seocho-gu, Seoul (Seocho-dong)
  • Subway: Line 3, Nambu Terminal Station (남부터미널역); Line 2, Gyodae Station (교대역)
  • Highlights: Korea's largest comprehensive arts facility — remarkable architecture
  • Entry: Indoor performances/exhibitions have separate admission; outdoor photography is free
  • Visit tip: Night view is stunning (6–8pm)
  • How to get that shot: Use the building's geometric lines as a natural frame. Night lighting adds real grandeur

📸 How to Take Drama-Worthy Photos

🎬 3 Essential Composition Techniques

1. Rule of Thirds

  • What it is: Divide the frame into a 3×3 grid and place your subject at one of the intersections
  • On your phone: Camera app → Settings → enable "Grid"
  • Use it: Everywhere — this is the most versatile and reliable composition for any filming location
  • Example:
  • Subject on the left third, background (building/nature) in the right two-thirds
  • Face in the upper third, body below

2. Backlighting

  • What it is: Shoot with the light source behind your subject to create a silhouette effect
  • Best timing: Sunrise (5–6am) or sunset (5–7pm)
  • Effect: Amplifies the drama's sense of mystery, fate, and emotion
  • Use it: Beach spots (Youngjin Beach, Wolhwa Street), night scenes (Nokseongpyeong overpass)
  • Tip: Turn your face slightly to the side — let the light catch the edges of your hair

3. Framing

  • What it is: Use natural frames (windows, architecture, trees) to draw focus to your subject
  • Use it: Bukchon Hanok Village (windows/walls), Wolhwa Street (cafe entrances), Baedari Alley (stacks of books)
  • How: Position yourself so the surrounding lines and shapes naturally wrap around you

⚠️ 5 Mistakes to Avoid

1. Cropping at joints: Don't cut off at the ankle, knee, waist, or neck — shoot full body or from mid-thigh up

2. Merging with the background: Make sure poles or branches don't pass through your head or neck

3. Digital zoom: Smartphone digital zoom degrades image quality — walk closer instead

4. Flat composition: Create depth by arranging foreground, midground, and background as distinct layers

5. Forced poses: Leaning, sitting, and resting naturally beats any awkward stretch

🎫 Practical Tips & Etiquette

Things to Keep in Mind at Filming Locations

1. Respect private property

  • Some filming locations are regular stores, homes, or schools — not public attractions
  • Example: Photograph schools from outside only; be quiet in residential alleys; buy something at cafes you visit
  • Etiquette: Behave as a respectful visitor, not just a quick photo-taker

2. Check operating hours

  • Museums, the Seoul Arts Center, and similar venues have separate admission fees and hours — always check
  • Residential neighborhoods: Visit quietly between 10am and 5pm

3. Some spots may have restricted access

  • Depending on drama popularity, some locations may have photo restrictions or may have closed
  • Check the official website or a recent blog post before visiting

🌟 Final Tip: Shoot with the Drama's Emotion

> A photo isn't just a record — it's capturing the moment when you become the main character of that scene.

3-Step Emotional Immersion:

1. Recall the scene (2 minutes)

  • Before you shoot, rewatch the relevant drama scene on YouTube or your streaming app
  • Remember the character's emotion, the music, the weather, the lighting

2. Recreate that feeling (1 minute)

  • Recreate the character's expression and body language with your own
  • Ask yourself: "What would this character's face look like right now?"

3. Embed the story in the photo

  • Add a drama quote or scene description in your caption
  • When sharing with fan communities, include your emotional interpretation alongside the photo
