Three Mistakes International Visitors Most Often Make

1. Believing "Daiso (다이소) covers everything"

Daiso (다이소) is unbeatable on value but only carries 30~50 diary SKUs. 10x10 (텐바이텐), Kyobo Book Centre (교보문고) and Young Poong Bookstore (영풍문고) each stock 200+ Korean-design diaries — for serious diary hunters, Daiso (다이소) alone falls short.

2. Missing the diary season (October–December)

Korea's diary calendar runs September to December: new releases drop, limited editions launch, popular SKUs sell out. By January–February the best diaries and pens are gone. If diary shopping is the trip's purpose, fall is the timing.

3. Treating Insadong (인사동) as the catch-all for traditional stationery

Insadong (인사동) is strong for traditional brushes, ink, hanji (한지), and calligraphy supplies, but weak for modern diaries, pens, and design stationery. Going only to Insadong (인사동) for diaries or fountain pens is a wasted trip — head to Gwanghwamun (광화문) Kyobo or Gangnam Yeongpoong / Morris instead.

① Kyobo Book Centre Gwanghwamun (교보문고 광화문점) — Korea's Diary & Pen Headquarters

Location: B1, Kyobo Life Building, Jongno 1, Jongno-gu, Seoul — Subway Line 5, Gwanghwamun Station (광화문역) Exit 4, directly connected

Why here:

  • Korea's largest bookstore. The Gwanghwamun (광화문) flagship occupies a full B1 floor of books + stationery
  • 200+ diaries and 1,000+ pens in one walkable space
  • In-store Pen Showroom (글방) — try fountain pens, rollerballs, and ink before buying

Must-buy categories (as of 2026-04):

  • Korean design diaries: Motemote (모트모트), Indigo (인디고), Arte (아르테) — ₩15,000~30,000
  • Calligraphy pens: Sakura, Dolphins (돌핀스) — ₩2,000~10,000 each
  • English-edition Korean design notebooks: Motemote (모트모트) Global line — ₩12,000~25,000
  • K-Books in English: Korean literature in translation — ₩15,000~25,000
  • Hanji (한지) cards & bookmarks: ₩2,000~8,000

Tips:

  • Hours: 09:30 ~ 22:00
  • Foreigner membership signup → 5% off + points
  • The deepest aisle hides a limited-edition / artist-collab corner updated monthly
  • Tax refund desk inside store for receipts ₩30,000+

② Young Poong Bookstore Jongno (영풍문고 종각종로본점) — The Imported-Stationery Powerhouse

Location: B1~B2, Yeongpoong Building, 41 Cheonggyecheon-ro, Jongno-gu, Seoul — Subway Line 1, Jonggak Station (종각역) Exit 4, 1-min walk

Why here:

  • Where Kyobo (교보) leads on Korean design, Young Poong (영풍) leads on imports — Japan's MUJI / Midori / Kokuyo / Life, Germany's Lamy / Faber-Castell
  • Two floors (B1, B2) with B2 dedicated to stationery
  • Familiar global brands + Korea-exclusive collabs

Must-buy categories:

  • Midori Traveler's Notebook (Korea-exclusive colors): ₩30,000~80,000
  • Lamy Safari fountain pen: ₩40,000~70,000 (often cheaper than overseas)
  • Leuchtturm1917 dot-grid notebook: ₩28,000~35,000
  • Kokuyo Campus notebook (Korea-exclusive design): ₩3,000~8,000
  • Daily diary in English: ₩15,000~30,000

Tips:

  • Hours: 10:00 ~ 22:00
  • The Gangnam COEX branch is comparable — pick by route
  • New imports typically restock once per season (spring / fall) — miss the window and wait

③ 10x10 (텐바이텐) Hongdae — #1 for Design Stationery

Location: 138 Eoulmadang-ro, Mapo-gu, Seoul — Subway Line 2, Hongik University Station (홍대입구역) Exit 8, 5-min walk (Daehakno (대학로) branch also operates)

Why here:

  • The pioneer of Korean design select-shops — online since 1999, the offline store is a foreign hunter's #1 stop
  • Unmatched range of Korean illustrator / designer collab notebooks
  • Both characters (Kuromi, Chiikawa, My Melody Korea-exclusive collabs) and designer diaries

Must-buy categories:

  • Korean designer collab notebooks: ₩8,000~20,000
  • Character diaries (Korea-exclusive): ₩15,000~30,000
  • Illustration memo pads / sticker sets: ₩3,000~10,000
  • Travel / handy-size diaries: ₩10,000~20,000
  • Eco-paper notebooks (Korean design): ₩8,000~15,000

Tips:

  • Hours: 11:00 ~ 22:00 (Hongdae)
  • Daiso (다이소) vs 10x10 (텐바이텐): ₩1,000~3,000 character items at Daiso (다이소) vs ₩8,000~20,000 designer items at 10x10 (텐바이텐) — value vs curation trade-off
  • The in-store bestseller corner is the fastest path to Korea's Instagram-popular diaries

④ ARTBOX (아트박스) Myeongdong — Art · Stationery · Character All-in-One

Location: 14 Myeongdong-gil, Jung-gu, Seoul — Subway Line 4, Myeongdong Station (명동역) Exit 6, 2-min walk

Why here:

  • Korea's homegrown design lifestyle chain. The Myeongdong (명동) store has a high foreign-customer share
  • Art goods + stationery + characters + interior accessories in one store
  • Price band sits between Daiso (다이소, low) and 10x10 (텐바이텐, mid) — affordable design

Must-buy categories:

  • Korean illustrator notebooks / diaries: ₩5,000~18,000
  • Character keyrings / stickers: ₩3,000~8,000
  • Lifestyle accessories (bags, pouches): ₩10,000~30,000
  • Korean-design masking tape: ₩2,000~5,000
  • Mini art posters: ₩5,000~15,000

Tips:

  • Myeongdong (명동) / Hongdae (홍대) / Gangnam (강남) branches share most of the lineup — pick by your route
  • During diary season (Oct–Dec), a separate limited-edition curation block appears

⑤ Morris (모리스) Gangnam — The Fountain Pen Mecca

Location: Around Gangnam Station (강남역), Gangnam-gu, Seoul (check the official site or Naver Map for exact branch)

Why here:

  • The pilgrimage destination for Korean fountain pen enthusiasts. Lamy / Pelikan / Pilot / Sailor and more — a global pen carrier
  • Try-out desks — dip and write before you buy
  • Korea's widest selection of fountain pen ink colors (300+ SKUs)

Must-buy categories:

  • Lamy Safari / Al-Star: ₩40,000~80,000
  • Pilot Kakuno / Metropolitan: ₩30,000~60,000
  • Pelikan Classic M200 / M205: ₩200,000+
  • Bottled ink (full color range): ₩15,000~50,000
  • 5 ml ink-sample sets: ₩5,000~15,000

Tips:

  • The strongest reason for a foreigner to stop here: Korea-exclusive ink colors rarely sold abroad
  • Korean ink brands — WAESL, Forever Pen — have colors found nowhere else
  • Try a pen first; nib feel matters
  • Pair with Homi Hwabang (호미화방) (next destination) by splitting Insadong (인사동) and Gangnam (강남) into two trips

⑥ Homi Hwabang (호미화방) Insadong — Traditional Art Supplies + Calligraphy

Location: Around Insadong (인사동), Jongno-gu, Seoul — Subway Line 3, Anguk Station (안국역) Exit 6, 5-min walk

Why here:

  • Founded in 1957 — among Korea's oldest art-supply stores
  • Traditional brushes, ink, hanji (한지), pigments, and calligraphy gear in one place
  • A regular stop for foreigners taking calligraphy / drawing classes

Must-buy categories:

  • Korean traditional brushes (hopil / nangohopil / 호필 · 낭호필): ₩5,000~80,000
  • Ink stick / liquid ink / inkstone sets: ₩10,000~50,000
  • Hanji (한지) (assorted colors and weights): ₩1,000~10,000 per sheet
  • Calligraphy paper bundles (hwaseonji / 화선지): ₩15,000~40,000
  • Watercolor / acrylic pigments (Korean & Japanese brands): ₩3,000~30,000

Tips:

  • Hours: weekday 09:00 ~ 19:00, weekend shorter
  • Tell the staff "for a foreign friend" — they'll suggest curated picks
  • Within a 3-min walk of Insadong Ssamziegil (인사동 쌈지길) — combine the two

⑦ Jongno 4-ga Stationery Street (종로4가 문방구 거리) — Wholesale · Low-Price · Local

Location: Around Jongno 4-ga (종로4가), Jongno-gu, Seoul — Subway Line 1, Jongno 5-ga Station (종로5가역) Exit 6, 5-min walk

Why here:

  • The traditional wholesale street for Korean student stationery. Office supplies, exam papers, notebooks at wholesale prices
  • The best price in town for Monami 153 (모나미 153) ballpoint pens and other classic Korean writing tools
  • A living slice of Korean student culture — Instagram / blog photogenic

Must-buy categories:

  • Monami 153 (모나미 153) ballpoint: ₩300~500 each (₩200 each in bulk)
  • Monami Plus Pen (모나미 플러스펜): ₩500~1,000 each
  • Classic Korean notebooks (university / practice): ₩500~3,000
  • Student set (ruler, protractor, compass): ₩3,000~10,000
  • Wholesale old-school diaries / notebooks: ₩2,000~8,000

Tips:

  • Hours: weekday 09:00 ~ 18:00 (many shops closed Sundays)
  • Few foreign visitors — Korean-language signage, cash strongly preferred
  • A dozen Monami 153 (모나미 153) ballpoints (12 pens) for ₩4,000~5,000 makes a no-brainer souvenir

⑧ MUJI Korea (무인양품) Gangnam — Korea-Only Diaries

Location: Around Sinnonhyeon (신논현) / Gangnam Station (강남역), Gangnam-gu, Seoul (check the official site for exact branch)

Why here:

  • Global MUJI is familiar — Korea's edge is Korea-only limited-edition diaries and stationery
  • Occasional Korean-designer collab series
  • The first stop for foreigners hunting Korea-exclusive SKUs unavailable back home

Must-buy categories:

  • MUJI diary in Korea-only colors: ₩12,000~25,000
  • MUJI notebooks (Korean design): ₩3,000~10,000
  • Pen + refill Korea sets: ₩3,000~8,000
  • Stationery storage boxes (Gangnam-flagship spec): ₩5,000~30,000
  • Korea-exclusive textile / tray: ₩10,000~50,000

Tips:

  • Hours: 11:00 ~ 22:00
  • Global MUJI membership works in Korea — link your home card
  • Gangnam (강남) is the largest; Myeongdong (명동) / Hongdae (홍대) are smaller

FAQs from International Visitors

Q1. When is the best time for diary shopping?

Mid-October to mid-December. Some new releases land in September; limited editions launch from mid-October; popular models sell out by mid-December. January–February is mostly out of stock until the next season.

Q2. Can I buy Korean diaries from abroad?

Some brands (Motemote / 모트모트, Midori) ship globally, but limited editions are Korea-only. Returns are also tricky — buying in person is safer.

Q3. First fountain pen — where to start?

Try Lamy Safari (₩40,000) or Pilot Kakuno (₩30,000) at the Morris (모리스) Gangnam try-out desk. Cheaper pens have inconsistent ink flow; ₩200,000+ is overkill for a first pen.

Q4. Bringing hanji (한지) / brushes home — any issues?

Hanji (한지) and brushes ride fine in carry-on or checked baggage. Liquid ink is classified as liquid — checked baggage only. Pack large bottles carefully.

Q5. Where can I pay / ask in English?

Kyobo Gwanghwamun (교보문고 광화문) / Young Poong Jongno (영풍문고 종로) / 10x10 Hongdae (텐바이텐 홍대) / ARTBOX (아트박스) / MUJI (무인양품): English OK. Homi Hwabang (호미화방) / Jongno 4-ga (종로4가): Korean-leaning.

Q6. Are there diaries with English printing?

Motemote (모트모트) / Indigo (인디고) run Global / English lines. Check stock at Kyobo Gwanghwamun (교보문고 광화문). Global brands (Midori, Leuchtturm) are entirely English.

Q7. Daiso (다이소) vs 10x10 (텐바이텐) vs ARTBOX (아트박스) — what's the difference?

  • Daiso (다이소): ₩1,000~3,000 / character & souvenir / see #52
  • ARTBOX (아트박스): ₩5,000~18,000 / design + character / value-design balance
  • 10x10 (텐바이텐): ₩8,000~25,000 / Korean emerging-designer collabs / Instagram-popular
  • For serious diary shopping: 10x10 (텐바이텐) or Kyobo (교보). For value: Daiso (다이소).

Q8. Where do I get Tax Refund?

Kyobo (교보문고) / Young Poong (영풍문고) / ARTBOX (아트박스) / 10x10 (텐바이텐) / MUJI (무인양품): instant refund inside the store. Morris (모리스) / Homi Hwabang (호미화방) / Jongno 4-ga (종로4가): varies by store — confirm at checkout.

Seasonal Notes

Spring (March – May)

  • Imported-stationery spring releases at Young Poong (영풍) / Kyobo (교보) — April is best
  • New-semester crowds at the wholesale street

Summer (June – August)

  • Diary off-season — few new releases
  • Stores quieter, ideal for try-outs and demos

Autumn (September – November)

  • Diary new-release surge (mid-October on)
  • Kyobo (교보) and 10x10 (텐바이텐) limited-edition curation blocks open
  • The peak season for foreign diary hunters

Winter (December – February)

  • Last picks for limited editions through mid-December
  • January–February is wait-mode — better timing for fountain pen / calligraphy gear
