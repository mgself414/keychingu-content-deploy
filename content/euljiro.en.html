Why Euljiro? Here's the Deal

"The Real Seoul Where Past & Present Overlap"

Euljiro is the most contradictory neighborhood in Seoul. Weathered steel workshops, printing houses, and parts shops still operate, yet right next door sit trendy bars, craft cocktail specialists, and vintage restaurants in harmony.

  • Converted Building Culture: 1970s factory buildings kept as-is, with modern cafes and bars inserted inside
  • Authenticity: Unlike Seongsu (성수, which feels "curated"), Euljiro retains genuine traces of labor and craftsmanship
  • Time-Based Transformation: See actual artisans working during the day; at night, the street becomes something entirely different
  • Modern Korean History: The most vivid remnants of Korea's 1960~70s economic development era

Especially for English-speaking travelers, Euljiro is the definition of "authentic Seoul" — not an Instagram playground, but a neighborhood built by real Seoulites.

Euljiro's Three Faces: Day · Evening · Night

Daytime (10 AM~3 PM) — Working Seoul

This is a time visitors should NOT skip — it's the only chance to see Euljiro's true identity.

What You'll See Details
Steel Workshops Opposite side of alleys: lathes, welding sparks, smoke. Real Korean manufacturing floor
Printing Alleys Euljiro 4-ga (을지로4가) area: offset print shops still operating. Ink smell present
Metal Parts Shops Weathered signs on shops still open. Peeking inside is content gold
Old-School Restaurants Hand-written menus, worn tables, elderly proprietors. ₩10,000~15,000 meals
Artisans at Work High probability of seeing people actively working

Tip: Don't point cameras at people. Just walk and observe. People dislike being photographed without permission.

Evening (4 PM~9 PM) — The Street Transforms

This is the time that made Euljiro a "hotspot." Office workers and tourists blend; the street starts its metamorphosis.

Time Vibe Recommended Activity
4~6 PM Day and night mix Cafe visit or vintage building exploration
6~8 PM Gradually darkens, neon signs light up Bar entry begins, drinking possible
8~10 PM Full night street Alcohol culture in full swing, crowds increase

Night (9 PM~Midnight) — Euljiro's True Face

Euljiro's night view: Old buildings studded with vintage neon signs, warehouse lights, alleys full of people.

No longer a "place to see" — it becomes a place to experience.

5 Must-Visit Spots

1. Euljiro 3-ga Printing Alley & Craft Cocktail Bar — "Noise Maker"

Location: Euljiro 3-ga 51-1 vicinity (Exit 2, Euljiro 3-ga Station)

Hours: Opens from 6 PM, until 11 PM (Thu~Sun)

Features:

  • Sophisticated cocktail bar hidden inside a weathered printing building
  • Minimal interior: exposed concrete, industrial pipes, parts of old printing machines remain
  • Speakeasy vibe: entrance barely noticeable

Recommended Order:

  • Negroni (basic cocktail, Euljiro signature)
  • Seasonal cocktails (rotate seasonally)
  • Price: ₩15,000~22,000

Experience:

  • The contrast of sipping refined cocktails in a shabby space embodies Euljiro's identity itself
  • Bartender is friendly (but atmosphere demands quiet)

Tip: Best to make a reservation (Instagram DM or in-person inquiry).

2. Euljiro 4-ga Old-Fashioned Soup Restaurant & Pojangmacha — "Working People's Table"

Location: Euljiro 4-ga area (near Euljiro 4-ga Station (을지로4가역))

Hours: 11 AM~2 PM / 6 PM~10 PM

Features:

  • Not a destination for "delicious food," but for experiencing real Euljiro
  • Hand-written menus, 30+ year-old signs, worn stainless steel tables
  • Few pojangmacha (포장마차, street food tents) nearby; worth stopping at one or two

Menu:

  • Soup rice (beef tripe, ox bone, spicy beef): ₩10,000~15,000
  • Hand-cut kalguksu (칼국수) noodles: ₩8,000~10,000
  • Tonkatsu (pork cutlet): ₩15,000

Experience:

  • Laborers, construction workers, office employees eat together
  • Ordering with hand gestures to a non-English-speaking proprietor
  • Real Korean daily dining

Tip: Lunch rush (11:30~1:00) is very crowded. 2~3 PM is quietest.

3. Euljiro Dried Squid Snack Hof — "Korean Outdoor Pub Culture"

Location: Intersection of Euljiro 3-ga and Euljiro 4-ga area (multiple locations)

Hours: 5 PM~11 PM

Features:

  • Entire 2nd floors converted into dried squid snack hof exclusively
  • Traditional "gamaek" (가맥, store beer) version
  • White bib-wearing, squatting-down drinking culture

Menu:

  • Draft beer (500ml): ₩4,000~5,000
  • Squid (premium): ₩8,000~12,000
  • Assorted sides (egg roll, sausage, vegetables): ₩3,000~8,000

Experience:

  • Drinking in the same space as Korean office workers
  • Euljiro night view from a 2nd-floor weathered building
  • Enjoyable without language barriers — the most authentically Korean drinking culture

Caution: Weekends after 8 PM are very crowded. Weekday evening is more comfortable.

4. Euljiro Cafes & Art Galleries — "Weathered Buildings Reborn"

Unnamed Cafe: Art Gallery & Independent Publisher Combined

Location: Euljiro 3-ga alleys (exact address: check on-site)

Hours: 12 PM~8 PM (Closed Mondays)

Features:

  • 1950s building's 3~4 floors converted into cafes
  • Galleries, independent publishers, artist studios share the space
  • Steep stairs, exposed concrete walls

Drinks:

  • Specialty coffee: ₩7,000~10,000
  • Alternative beverages: ₩6,000~8,000
  • Cake: ₩6,000~9,000

Experience:

  • Climbing through the building, gradually transitioning to modern spaces
  • Each floor has different galleries/publishers/studios — multiple pass-throughs possible
  • Experience real "artists living in Euljiro"

Tip: Stairs take 20+ minutes to navigate. Sneakers essential.

5. Euljiro Night View Spot — "The Neon-Sign Street"

Location: Euljiro 3-ga, Euljiro 4-ga intersection, alleys above Gwang-ro (광로)

Optimal Time: 7 PM~9 PM (after sun fully sets, neon lights on)

Features:

  • Old shops' neon signs: red, blue, yellow
  • Buildings weathered, but lighting creates cinematic atmosphere

Photo Spots:

1. North alley from Euljiro 3-ga Station Exit 2 (printing alley)

2. Pojangmacha street near Euljiro 4-ga Station

3. Alleys near intersection with Gwang-ro

Tip: Atmosphere improves with darkness, but safety: don't walk alone late at night.

Common Visitor Mistakes

1. Taking Too Many Photos

Euljiro's value isn't "photography-worthy street" but "the feeling of standing there."

Walk without constantly raising your camera.

2. Hopping Every Shop

Euljiro isn't sprawling, but hitting every place = 5~6 hours.

Visit 3~4 places deeply instead of many shallowly.

3. Daytime Only

Euljiro's identity lies in the day-to-night contrast.

Visiting only during the day = seeing half of Euljiro.

4. Solo After 10 PM

Euljiro becomes club/karaoke culture after 9 PM.

If solo, daytime visits recommended.

FAQ

Q1: English Really Doesn't Work?

A: Correct, most small shops/bars don't speak English.

Workaround:

  • Download Google Translate app in advance
  • Point at menu with finger
  • Bartenders: ~20% speak English

Q2: Is It Dangerous?

A: Weathered neighborhood, but safer than Myeongdong (명동) or Hongdae (홍대).

Cautions:

  • Avoid after 11 PM (club culture)
  • Don't walk solo through alleys
  • Keep valuables close

Q3: Do I Have to Drink?

A: Of course not. Euljiro is about "experiencing the street," not drinking.

Alternatives:

  • Drink coffee at cafes
  • Fill up on soup rice/noodles
  • Just observe the night view

Q4: Day or Night — Which Is Better?

A: Different. Depends on your goal:

Goal Recommended Time
Understand Seoul's history + present Daytime (10 AM~2 PM)
Life photo Evening (5~7 PM)
Local culture experience Evening (6 PM~9 PM)
Night view photography Night (7~8 PM)

Euljiro's Future

Euljiro is currently facing gentrification risk.

Old buildings are being torn down one by one; new structures rising.

Now is the time to experience Euljiro "for real."

In 5 years, Euljiro may be completely different.

Final Advice

Euljiro is NOT a place requiring "planned tourism."

  • Don't open maps constantly
  • Don't search for specific Instagram spots
  • Just walk and feel it

Weathered buildings, old signs, working people, trendy cafes, neon lights at night.

All of it together creates the "Euljiro" experience.

That's why you should visit.
