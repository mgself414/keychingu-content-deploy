Why Gwangjang Market (광장시장) 2F?

"A Pattern-Dress Heaven Hidden Above the Food Market"

Gwangjang Market (광장시장, "Wide Storage Market") is Korea's most famous food market, world-renowned for the foreigner queues snaking around bindaetteok (빈대떡, mung-bean pancake), mayak gimbap (마약김밥, "addictive" mini rice rolls), and yukhoe (육회, beef tartare). That 1F scene dominates Instagram.

But the floor above is almost a different universe. Roughly 200 ~ 300 imported-vintage shops have lined the 2F since the 1980s, making it a parallel pilgrimage site for Korean MZ vintage diggers. Foreign visitors are rare here, which is exactly why Korean fashion enthusiasts call it their "secret digging spot."

KeyChinGu recommends Gwangjang's 2F separately from Dongmyo (동묘) for four reasons:

1. Specialized in women's wear — patterns and dresses — Where Dongmyo (동묘) leans on clothing-graveyard piles (옷 무덤), Gwangjang 2F's strength is patterned dresses, retro blouses, tweed jackets, plus rich autumn / winter outerwear (denim jackets, M-65-style fishtail parkas, coats).

2. Shop-format digging — no clothing graveyard — Everything is on hangers, sorted by size and item type. Far easier than Dongmyo (동묘)'s street stalls.

3. One minute to the food market — Finish digging, walk down the stairs, and you're in front of mayak gimbap (마약김밥) and bindaetteok (빈대떡). No comparable single-stop combination at Dongmyo (동묘).

4. Jongno 5-ga is on the Korea Tourism 100 — 1F food + 2F vintage + walking distance to Dongdaemun (동대문) and Nakwon Arcade (낙원상가). The lowest "lost foreigner" risk for vintage-curious first-timers.

Understanding Gwangjang's Three-Layer Layout

Gwangjang Market (광장시장) is not a single-floor market. Different markets stack on each floor and wing:

Floor Wing Specialty Foreigner POV
1F East Food alley (먹자골목) Bindaetteok (빈대떡), mayak gimbap (마약김밥), yukhoe (육회), janchi-guksu (잔치국수) Gwangjang's signature, the queue zone
1F West Hanbok (한복) · bedding · fabric Hanbok, traditional fabric, blankets Few foreigners, still curious
2F West Imported-vintage arcade (수입 구제상가) Vintage clothing The focus of this guide
2F East Accessories · misc Bags, watches, shoes Wholesale-leaning, low fit for tourists

The standard route: Exit 8 → straight ahead → through the food alley → past Manam Plaza (만남의 광장) → 2F staircase.

What Gwangjang's Vintage Arcade Does Best — Four Strengths

Strength 1 — Patterned Dresses (the essence of Korean vintage)

Korean fashion of the 1970s ~ 90s left behind bold floral, geometric, and paisley dresses, and this arcade has the deepest collection in Seoul. A category Dongmyo (동묘) barely carries.

  • Price: ₩10,000 ~ ₩40,000
  • Sizing: Korean-style (runs small) — "55" ≈ US S, "66" ≈ M
  • Use: Instagram photo looks, Korean spring / autumn picnic styling

Strength 2 — Retro Blouses + Skirt Sets

Tweed, button blouses, and A-line skirts imported from 90s Japan and Hong Kong fill the racks. Many shops let you build full set-up matches.

  • Price: 1 set-up ₩30,000 ~ ₩80,000
  • Best season: spring / autumn

Strength 3 — Autumn / Winter Outerwear (Denim, M-65, Coats)

The arcade's true edge is outerwear. Wholesale outerwear sourced from Japan, the US, and Europe — better-curated than Dongmyo (동묘).

  • Denim jacket: ₩15,000 ~ ₩40,000
  • M-65-style fishtail parka (야상): ₩30,000 ~ ₩60,000
  • Wool / cashmere blend coat: ₩50,000 ~ ₩150,000
  • Designer coat (Burberry, Maxmara, etc.): ₩100,000 ~ ₩500,000

Strength 4 — Luxury-Vintage Specialists (Burberry, YSL, Aquascutum, etc.)

Tucked deep inside the 2F, several shops specialize in luxury vintage. Similar position to Dongmyo (동묘)'s Salon de Bon (살롱드봉), but more shops here.

  • Price: ₩50,000 ~ ₩200,000 for a clean designer coat
  • Tip: Try "사장님 명품 있어요? (sajangnim, myeongpum isseoyo? — boss, do you have luxury items?)" — owners often pull premium pieces out of back storage.

Six-Step Digging Routine — Where It Differs from Dongmyo (동묘)

Step 1 — Pick your time

  • Weekday 10:00 ~ 12:00: KeyChinGu's #1. Fresh stock, low crowd, friendliest staff. Time to inspect sizing and condition.
  • Weekday 14:00 ~ 16:00: 1F food crowd has spilled upstairs. Digging is possible but the aisles feel tight.
  • Weekend: 1F food queues + 2F vintage crowd peak together. Not recommended.
  • Sunday: Over half the shops are closed. Skip.

Step 2 — Exit 8 → 2F entry

  • Jongno 5-ga Station (종로5가역) Exit 8 → straight 100 m → Gwangjang's west entrance (the hanbok / bedding lane).
  • At the entrance, ask "구제상가 어디예요? (gujesangga eodieyo? — where is the vintage arcade?)" — staff will point you.
  • Manam Plaza (만남의 광장) alley → stairs to 2F.

Step 3 — One full lap (calibrate prices)

Don't buy on first sight. Walk the full 2F first (~30 min) to calibrate. Patterned dresses average ₩10,000 ~ ₩40,000.

Step 4 — Pick a shop (and tell vintage from boutique)

The critical caution at Gwangjang 2F: not everything is vintage. Three categories coexist:

Type Tells Price
Imported vintage (수입 구제) Real secondhand, sourced from Japan / Europe / US ₩10,000 ~ ₩200,000
Boutique new (보세 신상) Brought in fresh from Dongdaemun (동대문) wholesale, vintage-styled ₩10,000 ~ ₩50,000
Counterfeit luxury (짝퉁 명품) Fake Burberry / LV labels "Designer" priced ₩50,000 ~ ₩200,000

ID tips:

  • Label check: Korean-language tag = boutique new. Japanese / English / Italian tag = likely imported vintage.
  • Garment condition: Subtle wear (slight neckband fade, button traces) = real vintage. Pristine = boutique new.
  • When unsure: ask "구제예요 보세예요? (gujeyeyo, boseyeyo? — vintage or boutique new?)" — honest owners answer truthfully.

Step 5 — Haggle + pay

  • Gwangjang 2F leans fixed-price. Haggle range is narrower than Dongmyo (동묘) — about 10 ~ 20%.
  • Bundle discounts (2 ~ 3+ items) are negotiable.
  • Card acceptance is higher than Dongmyo (동묘) — about 70%.
  • For small purchases (under ₩10,000), cash is appreciated.

Step 6 — 1F food alley for lunch

Wrap up digging, head down to 1F east, and finish with Gwangjang's signature foods. The "dig + eat in one stop" combo is Gwangjang's biggest edge.

Five 1F Foods — Quick Lunch After Digging

Gwangjang Market (광장시장) has its own dedicated food guide (see #15 Street Food Mecca); here we list only the five closest to the 2F arcade exit.

1. Bindaetteok (빈대떡, mung-bean pancake)

  • Where: 1F food-alley entrance (next to Manam Plaza / 만남의 광장)
  • Price: 1 piece ₩6,000 ~ ₩8,000
  • Menu: Plain bindaetteok, meat bindaetteok, kimchi bindaetteok

2. Mayak Gimbap (마약김밥, "addictive" mini rice rolls) — Monyeo Gimbap (모녀김밥), Gwangjang Gimbap (광장김밥), etc.

  • Where: Center of the food alley
  • Price: 8 pieces for ₩5,000
  • Note: Mini gimbap with yellow pickled radish + carrot. The #1 foreigner favorite at Gwangjang.

3. ₩2,000 All-You-Can-Eat Janchi-guksu (잔치국수, celebration noodles)

  • Where: End of the food alley
  • Same price band as Dongmyo (동묘); Gwangjang has its own version inside the food alley.

4. Yukhoe (육회, beef tartare) — Jamaejip (자매집), Buchon Yukhoe (부촌육회), etc.

  • Where: Inner part of Gwangjang's food alley
  • Price: 1 serving ₩15,000 ~ ₩25,000
  • Note: Korea's signature yukhoe (육회) hub. Foreign reactions vary.

5. Hotteok (호떡), Kkwabaegi (꽈배기, twisted donut), Doughnuts (Dessert)

  • Where: Food-alley exit
  • Price: 1 piece ₩1,500 ~ ₩3,000

KeyChinGu's verdict:

  • Beginner + women's wear + food in one → Gwangjang 2F
  • Serious digging + value + combo route (jjimjilbang + yajang) → Dongmyo (동묘)
  • Time to spare? Do both — Gwangjang in the morning, Dongmyo (동묘) in the afternoon. Two stops apart on Line 1, walkable too.

FAQs from International Visitors

Q1. Will anyone speak English?

The 1F food-alley owners often speak some English / Japanese / Chinese (high foreigner traffic). The 2F vintage arcade is mostly Korean-only. Sizes and prices come across through numbers, which is enough.

Q2. Should I visit on Sunday?

Not recommended. 1F food alley is open, but more than half of the 2F vintage shops are closed. Pick a weekday or Saturday.

Q3. What if I can't tell vintage from boutique-new?

Ask the owner directly + check the label + treat anything pristine as suspect. For shops asking ₩30,000+, when in doubt, walk to a different shop.

Q4. Should I buy counterfeit designer items?

Buying counterfeit goods inside Korea is technically legal, but customs in your home country may seize them (varies by country). Be cautious with luxury labels.

Q5. Can I try on sizes?

Some shops have a small fitting nook; others let you layer over your own outfit. Treat it like Dongmyo (동묘) — many shops have no mirror.

Q6. Can I do Gwangjang and Dongmyo (동묘) the same day?

Yes. Gwangjang 10:00 ~ 12:00 digging → 1F lunch → Line 1 two stops to Dongmyo Station (동묘앞역) → Dongmyo (동묘) afternoon digging is a popular full vintage day.

Seasonal Notes

Spring (March ~ May)

  • Pattern-dress new-stock season — go.
  • 1F food crowd starts to pick up.

Summer (June ~ August)

  • 2F has only partial AC; some shops feel hot.
  • Food beats digging.
  • Japanese / Greater-China visitor share peaks.

Autumn (September ~ November)

  • Gwangjang's best season — tweed, coats, parkas land in stock.
  • October weekday mornings = the year's golden window.

Winter (December ~ February)

  • Coat / padding season. Prices rise 30 ~ 50% over summer.
  • 1F food alley still packed.
