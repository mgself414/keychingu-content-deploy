So, What's a Gamaekjip (가맥집)?

"Ga-maek" — The Anti-Craft Beer Experience

A Gamaekjip is basically a pop-up pub squeezed into the corner of a neighborhood supermarket — think a dusty shop near a university or a local convenience store with a few plastic chairs shoved to the side.

What the word means:

  • Ga (가) = Gaga (Store)
  • Maek (맥) = Maekju (Beer)

But it's so much more than that. Here's how Gamaekjip compares to other drinking spots:

Location Trendy areas (Gangnam, Itaewon, Seongsu) Office districts (Yeongdeungpo, Jongno) Near universities, residential areas, markets

One-liner "Beer culture" experience "Work dinner" culture "Everyday life" experience

Why Is This So Special?

1️⃣ The Real Face of Everyday Korean Life

Seoul's "hot spots" are all over the tourist guidebooks. But where does the daily life of 25 million people actually happen?

Right here — at the Gamaekjip.

  • Office workers stop by the nearest supermarket after work and grab a beer
  • College students gather here after exams
  • Neighborhood aunties (아주머니) gossip over bottles at the local shop

As a tourist: The moment you walk into a Gamaekjip, you stop being a visitor. You're just another person living Seoul.

2️⃣ ₩2,000 for Beer + Friends + Good Conversation

Normally, drinking in Korea means:

  • Making a reservation
  • Picking from a menu
  • Waiting to order
  • Seeing the bill (disappointment incoming)

Gamaekjip is different:

1. Walk to the fridge and grab a beer (₩2,000~3,000)

2. Pick a snack from the shelf (₩3,000~8,000)

3. Start drinking immediately (wait time = 0 minutes)

And you're doing exactly what locals actually do.

3️⃣ The Antithesis of "K-Culture Tourism"

Most "K-culture tourism" goes like this:

  • Tea at a hanok (traditional Korean house) café
  • K-beauty shopping in Myeongdong
  • Taking photos at Instagram spots

But how Koreans actually drink is:

  • Not fancy cocktails
  • Not a curated "experience"
  • Just a can of beer, good company, and the night sky

Gamaekjip Visit Guide — The Real Etiquette

Step 1️⃣: Picking the Right Spot

🚫 Don't Go Here (No Tables)

Don't mistake a regular convenience store for a Gamaekjip — those are just places to grab a beer and go.

What to look for:

Condition Check
Tables and chairs inside? ✅ Essential (the store itself is the pub)
Clearly visible beer fridge? ✅ Essential (front and center, fully stocked)
Snack corner? ✅ Essential (dried seafood, chips, fruit)
Looks intimidating? ❌ Not a problem (older locals come too)
Listed in tourist guides? ❌ Actually avoid those (less authentic)

🎯 Look for Places Like This

```

Sign 1: An open, quiet neighborhood shop

Sign 2: Plastic chairs + small tables on the side or inside

Sign 3: Aged fridge packed with canned beers

Sign 4: A small eatery next door or below (good energy)

```

Step 2️⃣: Choosing Your Beer (The Fridge Culture)

Your first decision at any Gamaekjip happens in front of the fridge.

Beer Options (by price)

Beginner tips:

  • First round: Cass or Hite (safe and solid)
  • Summer: Cass (crisp and refreshing)
  • Winter: Hite (smoother and mellow)
  • Don't overthink it — just grab one like the locals do

Step 4️⃣: Ordering (Super Simple)

This is way easier than you think.

```

1. Grab 1~2 cans from the fridge

2. Pick 2~3 snacks from the shelf

3. Walk to the register and pay (show the amount / wait)

```

Payment Notes

Situation What to do
Card accepted? Ask first — almost always cash only
No cash? Find the nearest ATM (usually next to a convenience store)
Tax? Already included in the price
Drinking one can at a time? Totally fine — finish one, grab another

Step 5️⃣: Sitting Down and Drinking (Etiquette)

✅ Do This

  • Set your beer and snacks on the small table (don't hold everything in your hands)
  • Laugh naturally (it's a drinking spot after all)
  • Stay 2~3 hours (not too long)
  • Make eye contact with people nearby (a nod or smile is fine)
  • Clean up when you leave (wipe down the table, bin your trash)
  • Come after 8 PM (that's when it gets lively)

❌ Don't Do This

  • Shout or be loud (you'll stand out uncomfortably)
  • Take tons of photos (kills the vibe / people won't appreciate it)
  • Pester the staff (it's just a supermarket — they're busy)
  • Pay with credit card (causes problems)
  • Crowd a table meant for 2 with 4 people (respect the space)
  • Show up after 1 AM (closing time)

Top 5 Gamaekjip Neighborhoods

> 📍 The spots below are verified by locals as of 2024~2025.

> Hours and availability may change — check Naver Map or Diningcode before you go.

1️⃣ Euljiro 3-ga Area (The Mecca)

Why it's famous:

  • The birthplace of Seoul's Gamaekjip culture
  • Beautiful street lighting at night
  • A genuine gathering spot for Korean college students and office workers
  • Close to affordable hotels and famous restaurants

Notable spots:

  • Baengman-bul Sikpum (백만불식품) — Famous old-school Gamaekjip in the Euljiro 4-ga alley, known for pork rib snacks (featured on MBC's "Hang Out With Yoo"). Hours: 3 PM~11 PM, closed Sundays
  • Daeo Sikpum (대오식품) — Euljiro alley Gamaekjip with 25+ menu items (featured on MBC's "Oneul N")

Vibe:

  • Side streets transform into Gamaekjip row by night
  • Busiest 8 PM~11 PM
  • Led by the 20s and 30s crowd

Getting there:

  • Seoul Subway Line 2, Euljiro 3-ga Station (을지로3가역), Exit 5
  • Most places within 5 minutes on foot

2️⃣ Jongno 3-ga "Pimaek Alley" (The Classic)

Why it's special:

  • Old hanok buildings mixed with modern structures — seriously atmospheric
  • Deep-rooted Gamaekjip culture (many spots have been around 10+ years)
  • The night alley vibe is genuinely stunning

Notable spots:

  • Seoul Sikpum (서울식품) — Inside the Jongno 3-ga alley; ground floor shop + upstairs restaurant. Popular for Buchujeon (부추전, chive pancake) and Jeyuk-bokkeum (제육볶음, spicy pork stir-fry)
  • Sodaman Sikpum (소다만식품) — Near the Bossam alley in Jongno 3-ga; converted from an old supermarket

Vibe:

  • Near hostels (backpackers come here too)
  • Heavy on the 30s~40s office worker crowd
  • The cozy indoor warmth hits different on cold nights

Getting there:

  • Seoul Subway Line 1, Jongno 3-ga Station (종로3가역)
  • Alleys around Tapgol Park

3️⃣ University Districts (Hongdae / Konkuk / Seodaemun)

Why it's great:

  • The real daily life of young Koreans, unfiltered
  • Cheapest prices anywhere
  • Active late into the night

Area breakdown:

  • Hongdae (홍대): Sinchon and Ewha Womans University area (lots of art students and exchange students)
  • Konkuk (건대): In front of Konkuk University main gate (the heartbeat of student culture)
  • Seodaemun (서대문): Near Ewha Womans University

Tips:

  • Go on a weekday outside of exam season (less crowded)
  • Arrive around 9~11 PM (relaxed and easygoing)

4️⃣ Near Yeongdeungpo Market (Local Charm)

Why it's a hidden gem:

  • A real market atmosphere in the middle of the city
  • Tourists rarely come here — it's all locals
  • The energy of the market + the evening glow

Vibe:

  • Near Yeongdeungpo Electronics Market
  • Everyone from grandpas in their 60s to college kids in their 20s
  • Snacks feel fresh, like you just picked them up from the market

Getting there:

  • Seoul Subway Line 1, Yeongdeungpo Station (영등포역) / Line 5, Yeongdeungpo Market Station (영등포시장역)

5️⃣ Mangwon-dong / Hapjeong-dong (Rising Scene)

Why it's trending:

  • Gamaekjip culture has boomed here in the last 2~3 years
  • Perfect stop after a walk along the Han River
  • A mix of "Insta-worthy" vibes and genuine everyday life

Vibe:

  • Cleaner stores thanks to the influence of café culture
  • Close to Yeonnam-dong hotspots
  • Popular with 30-something office workers and international students

Tips:

  • Combine with a Han River cycling tour (perfect route)
  • You can show up as early as 7 PM and it's still good

👥 Is It Okay to Go Alone?

Absolutely, 100% natural.

You'll find plenty of:

  • Solo office workers
  • Solo college students
  • Solo grandpas

Tips for going solo:

1. Walk in with confidence (don't hesitate at the door)

2. Bring your phone or a book (easy way to fill solo time)

3. Making eye contact with people nearby is fine (the vibe is relaxed)

4. Staying more than 2 hours alone can get awkward — 1~1.5 hours is the sweet spot

💳 Payment Tips

❌ Credit cards

  • Almost no supermarkets accept them (old-school systems)
  • Even if they do, it's complicated

✅ Cash (essential)

  • Have 2~3 ₩10,000 bills on hand
  • Or a ₩5,000 note + coins combo
  • ATMs are nearby — prepare in advance

📱 Mobile payments

  • Some spots accept KakaoPay / NaverPay
  • But cash is always the safest bet

🌙 Going Late at Night

The real Gamaekjip is a night owl.

```

Best window: 9 PM~10:30 PM

  • Enough people around (not lonely)
  • Noise level is manageable
  • Still relaxed and easy
  • Shops are still open

```

📸 Taking Photos

Tips:

  • Keep it to one or two quiet shots (respect the atmosphere)
  • Don't capture other people's faces (privacy)
  • The street lighting at night is gorgeous (great for photos)
  • Beer can + snacks on the table = solid memory shot

Avoid:

  • Loudly calling people over to look at your camera
  • Mass-photographing staff or other customers
  • Live streaming or recording reels

FAQ

Q: I don't drink. Can I still go?

A: Of course! Plenty of people just grab chips or popcorn. They sell juice and water too. Just enjoy the vibe.

Q: Is it safe for a woman traveling alone?

A: Yes. It's just a neighborhood supermarket. That said, visiting between 9~11 PM (when more people are around) feels even more comfortable.

Q: Do I need to reserve?

A: Nope. Just walk in. There's no reservation system — it doesn't even exist here.

Q: What if I don't speak Korean?

A: You only need to pay, so you're completely fine. Just point:

  • Point at the beer
  • Point at the snack
  • Hand over the cash

Done!

Q: Can a Korean friend recommend a good one?

A: That's honestly the best way to find one. If you have a local Korean friend, just ask them.

Q: What's the difference between Gamaekjip and a regular bar?

A: Gamaekjip = a free-form, supermarket drinking session

Regular bar = fixed seats + set menu + structured atmosphere
